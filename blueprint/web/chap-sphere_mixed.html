14 Spherical converse, stage 2: mixed-sign curvature

14.1 Overview

Stage 2 removes global positivity from theorem 149: the prescribed geodesic curvature \(\kappa \) may be \(\le 0\) on part of the circle, provided the position-dependent admissibility \(\kappa (\theta )-\langle z(\theta ),ie^{i\theta }\rangle {\gt}0\) can be maintained. The pivotal observation (iter-048, sharpened iter-053 against the landed code) is that essentially the entire stage-1 machinery of chapter 13 is already sign-agnostic:

  • The four-vertex overlap window \(\bigl(\max (\kappa (q_1),\kappa (q_2)),\, \min (\kappa (p_1),\kappa (p_2))\bigr)\) has a positive upper part whenever the two maxima are positive — even when the minima are \(\le 0\). The small-contrast step levels \(a=c-h/2\), \(b=c+h/2\) are chosen inside that positive part, so the step model, the arc algebra, the first-variation expansion (lemma 207) and the boundary winding are literally the positive-stage objects. lemma 211 landed with hypotheses (Continuous, Periodic) only.

  • The transport layer (lemma 202, lemma 205) takes the curvature only through a pointwise lower bound \(\kappa \ge \kappa _0\) with no sign assumption on \(\kappa _0\). The margins lemma lemma 206 is relaxed in place from \(0{\lt}\kappa _0\) to \(-r^\ast (c){\lt}\kappa _0\) (see the re-sign note there: positivity entered its landed proof only through positivity of the slack \(m=\min (1-r^\ast ,\kappa _0+r^\ast )\)) — the quantitative version of the notes’ “\(\kappa _S{\gt}-R\)” lower range.

  • The whole S2-E chain — lemma 183, lemma 216, lemma 217, lemma 219 — landed in stage 1 with hypotheses (Continuous \(\kappa \), \(2\pi \)-periodic \(\kappa \), admissible closed trajectory) only: trajectory-speed positivity comes from the admissibility margin \(\ge \delta \), never from \(\kappa {\gt}0\). Stage 2 consumes these four declarations verbatim; no variant blocks exist or are needed.

Stage-2 prerequisites inside chapter 13 (Sphere.lean).

Two mechanical edits to the completed stage-1 file, both recorded as re-sign / visibility notes in the affected blocks: (i) relax the hypothesis of lemma 206 from \(0{\lt}\kappa _0\) to \(-r^\ast (c){\lt}\kappa _0\) (sole stage-1 consumer fixed up trivially); (ii) de-privatize lemma 165 and lemma 166 so the mixed assembly can use the same uniform-in-\(\kappa \) Lipschitz witnesses that broke the \(\varepsilon \)/\(L\)/\(h_1\) quantifier circularity in the positive assembly. Everything else in this chapter lives in the new file Gluck/SphereMixed.lean. The genuinely new Lean surface is: definition 220, lemma 224 (constant-shift reduction; the frozen Euclidean plateau engine lemma 27 keeps its positive hypothesis), lemma 225 (the assembly, mirroring the landed positive proof with a mixed-sign data step), and the capstone theorem 226.

  • Gluck.MixedSignSphereFourVertex

[mixed-sign spherical four-vertex hypothesis] A function \(\kappa :\mathbb R\to \mathbb R\) satisfies the mixed-sign spherical four-vertex hypothesis if it is continuous, \(2\pi \)-periodic, and either constant positive, or has the value-separated alternating extrema of definition 12 — four points \(p_1{\lt}q_1{\lt}p_2{\lt}q_2{\lt}p_1+2\pi \) with \(p_1,p_2\) local maxima, \(q_1,q_2\) local minima, and \(\max (\kappa (q_1),\kappa (q_2)){\lt}\min (\kappa (p_1),\kappa (p_2))\) — with both maxima positive, and moreover for some \(c\) in the positive part of the overlap window (\(\max \bigl(0,\kappa (q_1),\kappa (q_2)\bigr){\lt}c{\lt}\min (\kappa (p_1),\kappa (p_2))\)) the global lower bound

\[ \kappa (\theta )\; {\gt}\; -\bigl(\sqrt{1+c^2}-c\bigr)\qquad \text{for all }\theta \]

holds. (No global positivity: \(\kappa \) may be \(\le 0\) at and around the minima. The lower bound is the confinement-forcing condition: \(r^\ast (c)=\sqrt{1+c^2}-c\) is the model-circle radius, and \(\kappa {\gt}-r^\ast \) is what keeps the position-dependent denominator \(\kappa -\langle z,ie^{i\theta }\rangle \) positive along trajectories near the model. Compare MixedSignFourVertex in the Euclidean Dahlberg extension, where the analogous package is positivity at the two maxima only. Lean shape: mirror SphereFourVertex — a conjunction of Continuous, Periodic, and a disjunction whose non-constant branch bundles the four points, the two IsLocalMax / two IsLocalMin facts, the value separation with the extra \(0\) inside the \(\max \), and the existential \(c\) with the window membership and the global lower bound. Note \(\mathrm{SphereFourVertex}\; \kappa \to \mathrm{MixedSignSphereFourVertex}\; \kappa \): for positive \(\kappa \) any window midpoint works since \(-r^\ast {\lt}0{\lt}\kappa \); this implication is a cheap sanity lemma worth stating.)

[spherical converse, mixed sign] If \(\kappa \) satisfies the mixed-sign spherical four-vertex hypothesis (definition 220), then there is a simple closed curve \(z\) confined to the open disk realizing \(\kappa \) as its spherical geodesic curvature:

\[ \texttt{MixedSignSphereFourVertex }\kappa \; \Longrightarrow \; \exists \, z,\ \texttt{IsSimpleClosed }z\ \wedge \texttt{RealizesSphericalCurvature }z\, \kappa . \]

This subsumes theorem 149 (positive \(\kappa \) satisfies the mixed hypothesis trivially) and is the S² analogue of the Euclidean dahlbergConverse.

Proof ▶

The landed positive capstone proof (sphericalConverse_pos, end of Sphere.lean) with the mixed winding lemma substituted; every downstream ingredient landed sign-agnostic. Split the mixed-sign disjunction. The constant branch is constant positive by hypothesis: lemma 214 plus funext, no flow machinery. For the non-constant branch: lemma 225 supplies \(R{\lt}1\), \(\delta {\gt}0\), \(h_1\) (with derivative witness), and a closed admissible trajectory of the \((\kappa \circ h_1,R,\delta )\)-truncated flow; the flow specification (lemma 171) turns the flow value into the \(\mathrm{HasDerivWithinAt}\) trajectory hypotheses; lemma 183 (hypotheses: \(\kappa \circ h_1\) continuous and \(2\pi \)-periodic — exactly what the mixed data gives; positivity of the speed comes from the admissibility margin \(\delta \)) upgrades the periodic extension to a closed \(C^1\) curve confined to \(\{ |z|\le R\} \subset \{ |z|{\lt}1\} \) realizing \(\kappa \circ h_1\) in the gauge \(\varphi (\theta )=\theta \), and lemma 219 makes it simple. Pull back along \(\psi =h_1^{-1}\) from lemma 91 (\(C^1\) from the \(1/v(H\, t)\) derivative): lemma 215 transfers realization with \((\kappa \circ h_1)\circ H=\kappa \) by the left-inverse identity, and lemma 90 transfers simplicity.
