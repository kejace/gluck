14 Spherical converse, stage 2: mixed-sign curvature

14.1 Overview

Stage 2 removes global positivity from theorem 149: the prescribed geodesic curvature \(\kappa \) may be \(\le 0\) on part of the circle, provided the position-dependent admissibility \(\kappa (\theta )-\langle z(\theta ),ie^{i\theta }\rangle {\gt}0\) can be maintained. The pivotal observation (iter-048) is that the entire S2-D winding layer of chapter 13 is sign-agnostic and is reused verbatim:

  • The four-vertex overlap window \(\bigl(\max (\kappa (q_1),\kappa (q_2)),\, \min (\kappa (p_1),\kappa (p_2))\bigr)\) has a positive upper part whenever the two maxima are positive — even when the minima are \(\le 0\). The small-contrast step levels \(a=c-h/2\), \(b=c+h/2\) of lemma 209 are chosen inside that positive part, so the step model, the arc algebra, the first-variation expansion (lemma 205) and the boundary winding are literally the positive-stage objects.

  • The transport lemmas (lemma 200, lemma 203) already take the actual curvature only through a pointwise lower bound \(\kappa \ge \kappa _0\) with no sign assumption on \(\kappa _0\): the model margin hypothesis \(\langle z^\ast (\theta ),ie^{i\theta }\rangle \le \kappa _0-\delta -\mu \) is exactly the sign-changing admissibility in disguise. Along the near-centered-circle model, \(\langle z^\ast ,ie^{i\theta }\rangle \approx -r^\ast \), so the margin is satisfiable precisely when \(\kappa _0{\gt}-r^\ast \) (up to slack) — the quantitative version of the notes’ “\(\kappa _S{\gt}-R\)” lower range.

What genuinely changes: (a) the hypothesis package (definition 218); (b) the margins lemma must admit \(\kappa _0\in (-r^\ast ,c]\) instead of \((0,c]\) (lemma 221); (c) two positive-stage lemmas carry an unused positivity hypothesis (IsCurvatureFunction) that must not survive into stage 2. For lemma 181 the relaxation to “continuous and \(2\pi \)-periodic” is folded into the stage-1 S2-E re-sign (positivity of the speed comes from the admissibility margin, not from \(\kappa {\gt}0\); see the statement note there) — one declaration serves both stages, no variant block needed. For lemma 210 the Euclidean plateau engine lemma 27 is frozen with its positive hypothesis, so stage 2 uses the constant-shift reduction lemma 220 below — a genuine (small) new lemma, not an edit to frozen files.

Definition 218 mixed-sign spherical four-vertex hypothesis
#

Uses

  • Definition 12
  • Definition 147

Lean declarations

  • Gluck.MixedSignSphereFourVertex

A function \(\kappa :\mathbb R\to \mathbb R\) satisfies the mixed-sign spherical four-vertex hypothesis if it is continuous, \(2\pi \)-periodic, and either constant positive, or has the value-separated alternating extrema of definition 12 with both maxima positive (\(\kappa (p_1),\kappa (p_2){\gt}0\)), and moreover for some \(c\) in the positive part of the overlap window (\(\max (0,\kappa (q_1),\kappa (q_2)){\lt}c{\lt}\min (\kappa (p_1),\kappa (p_2))\)) the global lower bound

\[ \kappa (\theta )\; {\gt}\; -\bigl(\sqrt{1+c^2}-c\bigr)\qquad \text{for all }\theta \]

holds. (No global positivity: \(\kappa \) may be \(\le 0\) at and around the minima. The lower bound is the confinement-forcing condition: \(r^\ast (c)= \sqrt{1+c^2}-c\) is the model-circle radius, and \(\kappa {\gt}-r^\ast \) is what keeps the position-dependent denominator \(\kappa -\langle z,ie^{i\theta } \rangle \) positive along trajectories near the model. Compare MixedSignFourVertex in the Euclidean Dahlberg extension, where the analogous package is positivity at the two maxima only.)

Lemma 220 \(L^1\) step reparametrization without positivity
#

Uses

  • Lemma 210
  • Definition 19
  • Lemma 210

Lean declarations

  • Gluck.exists_step_L1_reparam_relaxed

Let \(\kappa \) be continuous and \(2\pi \)-periodic (no positivity), \(0{\lt}a{\lt}b\), and \(\theta _1{\lt}\theta _2{\lt}\theta _3{\lt}\theta _4{\lt}\theta _1+2\pi \) with \(\kappa (\theta _1,\theta _2,\theta _3,\theta _4)=(a,b,a,b)\). For every \(\varepsilon {\gt}0\) there is an orientation-preserving circle reparametrization \(h_1\) (\(\mathrm{StrictMono}\), \(C^1\) with continuous positive derivative, \(h_1(\theta +2\pi )=h_1(\theta )+2\pi \)) such that

\[ \int _0^{2\pi }\bigl|\kappa (h_1(\theta ))-\kappa ^\ast (\theta )\bigr|\, d\theta \; {\lt}\; \varepsilon , \qquad \kappa ^\ast =\texttt{stepCurvature } b\, a\, 0\, (\pi /2)\, \pi \, (3\pi /2). \]
Proof ▶

Constant-shift reduction to the positive-stage lemma; the frozen Euclidean plateau engine is applied only to a positive function. Pick \(M{\gt}\max \bigl(0,-\min _{[0,2\pi ]}\kappa \bigr)\) (finite by compactness and periodicity), so \(\kappa +M\) is a curvature function (continuous, \(2\pi \)-periodic, \({\gt}0\)). The crossing data shifts to \((\kappa +M)(\theta _k)=(a+M,b+M,a+M,b+M)\) with \(0{\lt}a+M{\lt}b+M\), so lemma 210 applied to \(\kappa +M\) with levels \(a+M,b+M\) yields \(h_1\) with \(\int _0^{2\pi }|(\kappa +M)\circ h_1-\kappa ^\ast _M|{\lt}\varepsilon \), where \(\kappa ^\ast _M=\texttt{stepCurvature}\, (b+M)\, (a+M)\, 0\, (\pi /2)\, \pi \, (3\pi /2)\). Pointwise \(\kappa ^\ast _M=\kappa ^\ast +M\) (the step function takes only the two level values, each shifted by \(M\)), so the integrand equals \(|\kappa \circ h_1-\kappa ^\ast |\) pointwise and the same \(h_1\) witnesses the claim. (\(\sim \)30–60 LOC; the only Lean content beyond the application is the pointwise shift identity for stepCurvature.)

Lemma 221 mixed-sign margin transport
#

Uses

  • Definition 219
  • Lemma 200
  • Lemma 203
  • Lemma 204

Lean declarations

  • Gluck.mixedSphere_margin_transport

Lemma 204 holds with the hypothesis \(\kappa _0\in (0,c]\) relaxed to \(\kappa _0\in (-r^\ast (c)+\sigma ,\, c]\) for any slack \(\sigma {\gt}0\) (with margins now depending on \(\sigma \)): explicit \(R{\lt}1\), \(\delta {\gt}0\), \(\mu {\gt}0\), \(\rho _0{\gt}0\), \(h_0{\gt}0\) such that the four quarter arcs of the concatenated step model near the centered circle satisfy \(\| z^\ast \| \le R-\mu \), \(\langle z^\ast (\theta ),ie^{i\theta }\rangle \le \kappa _0-\delta -\mu \), and clamp-inactivity. Consequently lemma 203 applies to mixed-sign \(\kappa \ge \kappa _0\) unchanged.

Proof ▶

Along the centered circle \(\langle z^\ast (\theta ),ie^{i\theta }\rangle \equiv -r^\ast \), so the required margin inequality reads \(-r^\ast \le \kappa _0-\delta -\mu \), i.e. \(\delta +\mu \le \kappa _0+r^\ast \), which has room exactly when \(\kappa _0{\gt}-r^\ast \); take \(\delta +\mu =\sigma /2\), and shrink \(\rho _0,h_0\) by the same Lipschitz-deviation argument as in lemma 204. The transport (lemma 200, lemma 203) never used \(\kappa _0{\gt}0\).
