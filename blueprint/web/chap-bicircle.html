5 Bicircle curves and their error vectors

This chapter analyses the geometry of the curves reconstructed from a four-arc step curvature \(\kappa _0\) (definition 19). Such a curve is a bicircle: four circular arcs, two cut from a circle of curvature \(a\) and two from a circle of curvature \(b\), joined so that the tangent line turns continuously through \(2\pi \). The main result is Dahlberg’s explicit formula (Proposition 9.1 of the source) for the error vector of a bicircle, which is the input to the winding argument of chapter 6.

  • Gluck.bicircleErrorVector

[Bicircle error vector] Source: DeTurck–Gluck, Proposition 9.1 (§9). Fix \(0 {\lt} a {\lt} b\) and breakpoints \(0 \le \theta _1 {\lt} \theta _2 {\lt} \theta _3 {\lt} \theta _4 {\lt} 2\pi \), and let \(\kappa _0\) be the four-arc step curvature (definition 19) taking the value \(b\) on \([\theta _1,\theta _2)\cup [\theta _3,\theta _4)\) and \(a\) elsewhere. The bicircle error vector is \(E_{\mathrm{bi}}(a,b,\theta _1,\dots ,\theta _4) = E(1/\kappa _0) = \int _0^{2\pi } e^{i\theta }/\kappa _0(\theta )\, d\theta \in \mathbb {C}\).

  • Gluck.bicircleErrorVector_eq

[Explicit error vector of a bicircle] Source: DeTurck–Gluck, Proposition 9.1 (§9), error-vector formula. With the notation of definition 28,

\[ E_{\mathrm{bi}}(a,b,\theta _1,\theta _2,\theta _3,\theta _4) \; =\; \Bigl(\tfrac {1}{ib} - \tfrac {1}{ia}\Bigr) \Bigl(\bigl[e^{i\theta _2} - e^{i\theta _1}\bigr] + \bigl[e^{i\theta _4} - e^{i\theta _3}\bigr]\Bigr). \]
Proof ▶

The weight \(\rho _0 = 1/\kappa _0\) is piecewise constant: it equals \(1/b\) on the two arcs \([\theta _1,\theta _2)\) and \([\theta _3,\theta _4)\) and \(1/a\) on the two complementary arcs. Split the integral \(E(\rho _0) = \int _0^{2\pi } e^{i\theta }\rho _0(\theta )\, d\theta \) into the four arcs. On each arc \(\rho _0\) is constant and \(\int _{c}^{d} e^{i\theta }\, d\theta = \frac{1}{i}\bigl(e^{id} - e^{ic}\bigr)\). Summing,

\[ E(\rho _0) = \tfrac {1}{ib}\bigl(e^{i\theta _2}-e^{i\theta _1}\bigr) + \tfrac {1}{ia}\bigl(e^{i\theta _3}-e^{i\theta _2}\bigr) + \tfrac {1}{ib}\bigl(e^{i\theta _4}-e^{i\theta _3}\bigr) + \tfrac {1}{ia}\bigl(e^{i(\theta _1+2\pi )}-e^{i\theta _4}\bigr). \]

Using \(e^{i(\theta _1+2\pi )} = e^{i\theta _1}\) and collecting the \(1/(ia)\) and \(1/(ib)\) terms, the four \(1/(ia)\) contributions sum to \(\tfrac {1}{ia}\bigl((e^{i\theta _3}-e^{i\theta _2}) + (e^{i\theta _1}-e^{i\theta _4})\bigr) = -\tfrac {1}{ia}\bigl((e^{i\theta _2} - e^{i\theta _1}) + (e^{i\theta _4} - e^{i\theta _3})\bigr)\), while the two \(1/(ib)\) contributions sum to \(\tfrac {1}{ib}\bigl((e^{i\theta _2} - e^{i\theta _1}) + (e^{i\theta _4} - e^{i\theta _3})\bigr)\). Hence the total is exactly \(\bigl(\tfrac {1}{ib} - \tfrac {1}{ia}\bigr)\bigl([e^{i\theta _2} - e^{i\theta _1}] + [e^{i\theta _4} - e^{i\theta _3}]\bigr)\), the stated form. (The source’s \(\bigl(\tfrac {1}{ia} - \tfrac {1}{ib}\bigr)\) is the same up to the overall sign, which only reflects the opposite labelling of the two curvature values on the arcs.)

[Arc integral of \(e^{i\theta }\)] Project-bespoke. For real \(c \le d\), \(\int _c^d e^{i\theta }\, d\theta = -i\bigl(e^{id} - e^{ic}\bigr)\).

Proof ▶

Fundamental theorem of calculus with antiderivative \(-i\, e^{i\theta }\) (whose derivative is \(e^{i\theta }\), using \(i\cdot i = -1\)).

[Step integrand is a.e. constant on an arc] Project-bespoke. On an arc \((c,d]\) on which \(\kappa _0 \equiv v\), the integrand \(e^{i\theta }\rho _0(\theta )\) agrees almost everywhere with \(e^{i\theta }(1/v)\) (the two single breakpoints are null).

Proof ▶

\(\rho _0 = 1/\kappa _0 = 1/v\) on the half-open arc; the endpoint where it may differ is a single point, of Lebesgue measure zero.

  • Gluck.bicircle_arc_integrable

[Per-arc integrability] Project-bespoke. The integrand \(e^{i\theta }\rho _0\) is interval-integrable on each of the five constant arcs.

Proof ▶

On each arc the integrand agrees a.e. with a continuous function (the constant weight times \(e^{i\theta }\)), which is interval-integrable; integrability transfers along a.e. equality.
