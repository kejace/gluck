\section{Overview}

This chapter assembles the $K$-generic \emph{space-form converse}: for
ambient curvature $K\in\{+1,-1,0\}$ (the disk model of $S^2$ at
$K=+1$, of $H^2$ at $K=-1$, and the flat plane in the
conformal gauge $\lambda=2$ at $K=0$), a curvature profile
satisfying the appropriate four-vertex admissibility hypothesis is realized as
the space-form geodesic curvature (\cref{def:spaceform_realizes}) of a simple
closed curve confined to the open unit disk. There are two stages, plus the
Euclidean instance of the positive stage
(\cref{thm:gluck_converse_spaceForm}), which yields a second proof of Gluck's
theorem.

\paragraph{Positive stage (\texttt{Reconstruction.lean},
\texttt{EndpointWinding.lean}, \texttt{Converse.lean}).}
\cref{thm:spaceform_converse_pos} is a genuine $K$-generic transport of
the spherical development of \cref{chap:sphere}: the same
endpoint-winding $\to$ reconstruction $\to$ simplicity pipeline, run once with the
sign $K$ carried through every estimate. The sign enters the winding
heart (\cref{thm:spaceform_endpoint_winding}) only through the conjugation
coefficient
\[
  \eta(K)\;=\;\frac{2\,r^\ast(K,c)\,K}{c^2+K},
  \qquad r^\ast(K,c)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{c^2+K}+c}
  \ \text{(\cref{def:centered_radius})},
\]
positive for $K=+1$, negative for $K=-1$, and
\emph{nonzero} at both curved signs (\texttt{stepError\_coeff\_ne\_zero});
since the winding replica
(\cref{lem:winding_conj_loop}) and the perturbation lemma
(\cref{lem:winding_number_c_perturb}) only see the \emph{norm} of the
conjugate-dominant term, the spherical winding argument transfers verbatim
across $K=\pm1$. The
hyperbolic phenomenon is the \emph{escape-velocity floor}: at
$K=-1$ the hypothesis carries $\kappa>1$ (curvature above a horocycle,
matching $\operatorname{coth}R>1$ for hyperbolic circles), which makes
$r^\ast(-1,c)=c-\sqrt{c^2-1}$ real and in $(0,1)$. The flat member
$K=0$ is different in kind: the gauge field
$q_{0,\kappa}(\theta,z)=1/(2\kappa(\theta))$ is independent of the position,
so the endpoint map is \emph{constant} in the start point, $\eta(0)=0$, and
the endpoint winding degenerates. Its non-constant branch instead closes by
the classical alignment winding in the $L^1$-quantitative form
\cref{lem:reduction_justified_L1}, with confinement supplied by an explicit
comparison of the reconstruction curve with the model circle of radius
$1/(2c)<1$ (the flat floor $\kappa>1/2$) — no flow, margins, or Gr\"onwall
transport needed: the flat case is the easy member. Instantiating
$K=+1$ recovers \cref{thm:spherical_converse_pos}; $K=-1$
yields the positive hyperbolic converse \cref{thm:hyperbolic_converse_pos};
$K=0$ feeds the second proof of Gluck's Euclidean theorem
(\cref{sec:two_proofs}).

\paragraph{Mixed stage (\texttt{MixedConverse.lean}).}
\cref{thm:spaceform_mixed_converse} removes global positivity, replacing the
hypothesis by \cref{def:mixed_sign_spaceform_four_vertex}, whose branches are
uniform in $K$ through the confinement threshold
$(1-K)/2$. Unlike the positive stage, its proof is \emph{not} an
independent $K$-generic construction: it is proved \textbf{by
reduction to the completed per-geometry capstones}. At $K=+1$ the
hypothesis \emph{is} the spherical \cref{def:mixed_sign_sphere_four_vertex}
(threshold $0$; identical confinement floor via
$r^\ast(1,c)=\sqrt{c^2+1}-c$, \texttt{centeredRadius\_one}), so
\cref{thm:spherical_converse} applies. At $K=-1$ it is the
hyperbolic \cref{def:mixed_sign_hyperbolic_four_vertex} (threshold $1$, the
escape velocity; the guarded floor is vacuous — the $H^2$ arc-length route
needs none), so the exact-profile capstone
\cref{thm:hyperbolic_mixed_converse_exact} applies. At $K=0$
(threshold $1/2$) the non-constant branch strengthens the Euclidean mixed-sign
hypothesis \cref{def:mixed_sign_four_vertex}, and the flat instance
\cref{thm:spaceform_mixed_converse_flat} concludes in the \emph{dilation-free}
Euclidean predicate via the Dahlberg converse \cref{thm:dahlberg_converse} —
the confined flat statement is false in general (scale rigidity; see the
paragraph after \cref{thm:hyperbolic_dahlberg_converse}). An earlier plan
proved the mixed statement by a $K$-generic transport of the $S^2$
mixed flow development; that route was superseded by the $H^2$ arc-length
engine and the reductions recorded here. The $K=-1$ instance is
packaged separately as \cref{thm:hyperbolic_dahlberg_converse}, the hyperbolic
Dahlberg converse of the four vertex theorem (Dahlberg 2005, hyperbolic
transport).

\section{Declarations}

\begin{definition}\leanok
[periodic extension]
  \label{def:spaceform_periodic_extension}
  \lean{Gluck.SpaceForm.periodicExtension}
  For a curve $\gamma:\mathbb R\to\mathbb C$, the $2\pi$-periodic extension of its
  values on $[0,2\pi)$:
  \[
    Z(t)\;=\;\gamma\Bigl(t-2\pi\bigl\lfloor t/(2\pi)\bigr\rfloor\Bigr).
  \]
  Model-agnostic support definition (no $K$); it is $2\pi$-periodic
  (\texttt{periodicExtension\_periodic}) and agrees with $\gamma$ on the fundamental
  window.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}\leanok
[reconstruction: truncation removal]
  \label{lem:spaceform_reconstruction}
  \lean{Gluck.SpaceForm.reconstruction_realizes}
  \uses{def:spaceform_periodic_extension, def:spaceform_realizes,
        def:spaceform_flow, def:is_closed_curve}
  Let $|K|\le1$, $\kappa$ continuous and $2\pi$-periodic, $R<1$,
  $\delta>0$. If $\gamma$ solves the truncated field
  $F_{K,\kappa,R,\delta}$ on $[0,2\pi]$ (in the
  $\mathrm{HasDerivWithinAt}$ sense), stays admissible there —
  $\|\gamma(\theta)\|\le R$ and
  $\delta\le\kappa(\theta)-K\langle \gamma(\theta),ie^{i\theta}\rangle$ —
  and closes up, $\gamma(2\pi)=\gamma(0)$, then there is a closed curve $Z$ agreeing with
  $\gamma$ on $[0,2\pi]$ with $\mathrm{Realizes}\;K\;Z\;\kappa$
  (\cref{def:spaceform_realizes}). ($K$-generic transport of
  \cref{lem:reconstruction_ode}, stated at the consumed-interface level; $Z$ is
  the periodic extension of $\gamma$.)
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  \uses{def:spaceform_periodic_extension, def:spaceform_flow}
  \leanok
  On the admissible slab the truncation in $F_{K,\kappa,R,\delta}$ is
  inactive, so $\gamma$ solves the true ODE
  $\gamma'=q_{K,\kappa}(\theta,\gamma)\,e^{i\theta}$ with the space-form gauge
  speed $q_{K,\kappa}(\theta,z)=(1+K\|z\|^2)/
  \bigl(2(\kappa(\theta)-K\langle z,ie^{i\theta}\rangle)\bigr)$.
  Periodicity of $\kappa$ makes the shifted windows solve the same equation, and
  $\gamma(2\pi)=\gamma(0)$ closes the seam, so the extension $Z$ has a genuine
  $\mathrm{HasDerivAt}$ everywhere. Admissibility extends periodically, making
  the speed $q_{K,\kappa}(t,Z(t))$ continuous, $2\pi$-periodic and
  strictly positive ($|K|\le1$ and $R<1$ give
  $1+K\|Z\|^2>0$; the denominator carries the $\delta$-margin). Hence
  $Z$ is $C^1$, regular, confined to $\{\|z\|\le R\}\subset\{\|z\|<1\}$, and in
  the gauge $\varphi(t)=t$ the identity of \cref{def:spaceform_realizes} is
  exactly the solved ODE.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\leanok
[space-form endpoint winding]
  \label{thm:spaceform_endpoint_winding}
  \lean{Gluck.SpaceForm.spaceForm_endpoint_winding}
  \uses{def:is_curvature_function, def:spaceform_flow, def:centered_radius,
        lem:sphere_curvature_lower_bound, lem:exists_abab_levels,
        lem:step_L1_reparam, lem:winding_conj_loop,
        lem:winding_number_c_perturb, thm:existence_of_zero}
  Let $K\in\{+1,-1\}$ (genuinely two-sign: at $K=0$ the
  endpoint map is constant in $z_0$ and the winding degree of freedom is dead,
  $\eta(0)=0$ — the flat member closes through
  \cref{lem:reduction_justified_L1} instead), let $\kappa$ be a curvature
  function (\cref{def:is_curvature_function}) with the escape-velocity floor
  $\kappa>1$ when $K<0$, and let $p_1<q_1<p_2<q_2<p_1+2\pi$ satisfy the
  value separation
  $\max(\kappa(q_1),\kappa(q_2))<\min(\kappa(p_1),\kappa(p_2))$. Then there exist
  $0<R<1$, $\delta>0$, an orientation-preserving circle reparametrization $h_1$
  ($\mathrm{StrictMono}$, continuous, $h_1(\theta+2\pi)=h_1(\theta)+2\pi$, with a
  continuous positive derivative witness), a radius $r_0\ge0$ and
  $z_0\in\overline B(0,r_0)$ such that the trajectory of the
  $(K,\kappa\circ h_1,R,\delta)$-truncated flow
  (\cref{def:spaceform_flow}) through $z_0$ is \emph{closed}
  ($\Phi(z_0,2\pi)=z_0$) and \emph{admissible} on $[0,2\pi]$:
  $\|\gamma(\theta)\|\le R$ and
  $(\kappa\circ h_1)(\theta)-K\langle \gamma(\theta),ie^{i\theta}\rangle
  \ge\delta$. (Transport of \cref{thm:spaceform_endpoint_winding}.)
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
  \uses{lem:sphere_curvature_lower_bound, lem:exists_abab_levels,
        lem:step_L1_reparam, lem:winding_conj_loop,
        lem:winding_number_c_perturb, thm:existence_of_zero,
        def:centered_radius}
  \leanok
  The six-step spherical assembly with $K$ carried through.
  \emph{Data:} take $c$ the midpoint of the overlap window; a uniform lower
  bound $\kappa\ge\kappa_0$ comes from
  \cref{lem:sphere_curvature_lower_bound} at $K=+1$ and from the floor
  $\kappa>1$ at $K=-1$. The $K$-generic margins package
  (\texttt{stepModel\_margins}, \cref{chap:spaceform_core}) at $(c,\kappa_0)$
  fixes $R,\delta,\mu,\rho_0,h_0$; the first-variation expansion
  (\texttt{stepError\_expansion}) at $c$ fixes $\rho_1,\bar h,C$ and the nonzero
  conjugation coefficient $\eta(K)$
  (\texttt{stepError\_coeff\_ne\_zero}); choose $\rho$, then step contrast $h$,
  then levels $a=c-h/2$, $b=c+h/2$ inside the window. \emph{Boundary margin:} on
  $\|z_0-z_0^\ast\|=\rho$ the expansion makes the step-model endpoint error
  $\eta h\,\overline{(z_0-z_0^\ast)}$-dominant, of norm $\ge|\eta|h\rho/2$.
  \emph{Reparametrization:} \cref{lem:exists_abab_levels} supplies $(a,b,a,b)$
  crossing data and \cref{lem:step_L1_reparam} an $h_1$ with
  $\int_0^{2\pi}|\kappa\circ h_1-\kappa^\ast|<\varepsilon'$, the tolerance fixed
  against the \emph{uniform-in-$\kappa$} Lipschitz witness
  \texttt{truncatedField\_lipschitz\_uniform} (explicit constant
  $R/\delta+(1+R^2)/(2\delta^2)$, blind to the curvature — this breaks the
  $\varepsilon'/L/h_1$ quantifier circularity). \emph{Winding:} Gr\"onwall
  transport makes the endpoint loop over $\|z_0-z_0^\ast\|=\rho$ a norm-small
  perturbation of the conjugate loop, of winding $-1$ by
  \cref{lem:winding_conj_loop} and \cref{lem:winding_number_c_perturb} — the
  sign of $\eta(K)$ is irrelevant since only
  $\|\eta h\overline{w}\|=|\eta|h\|w\|$ enters. \emph{Zero and admissibility:}
  \cref{thm:existence_of_zero} produces an interior zero $z_0$ of the endpoint
  map, i.e.\ a closed trajectory; the master estimate at $z_0$ gives
  admissibility on $[0,2\pi]$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}\leanok
[constant branch: the model geodesic circle]
  \label{lem:spaceform_circle_realizes}
  \lean{Gluck.SpaceForm.spaceFormCircle_realizes}
  \uses{def:spaceform_realizes, def:centered_radius, def:is_simple_closed}
  Let $K\in\{+1,-1,0\}$ and let $c$ be admissible for $K$
  ($0<c$ if $K=+1$; $1<c$ if $K=-1$; $1/2<c$ if
  $K=0$, where the circle is the Euclidean circle of coordinate
  radius $1/(2c)$). Then the centered
  circle $\gamma(\theta)=(-r)\,ie^{i\theta}$ of radius $r=r^\ast(K,c)$
  (\cref{def:centered_radius}) is a simple closed curve with
  $\mathrm{Realizes}\;K\;\gamma\;(\kappa\equiv c)$. (Transport of
  \cref{lem:spherical_circle_realizes}.)
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  \uses{def:centered_radius}
  \leanok
  $r=r^\ast(K,c)\in(0,1)$ and $K r^2+2cr-1=0$
  (\texttt{centeredRadius\_mem\_Ioo}, \texttt{centeredRadius\_solves}). The
  circle is $2\pi$-periodic, injective on a period, $C^\infty$, regular, and
  confined; with gauge $\varphi(\theta)=\theta$ the realization identity
  $(1+K r^2)/2=r(c+K r)$ is a rearrangement of the defining
  quadratic.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}\leanok
[realization transfers under $C^1$ reparametrization]
  \label{lem:spaceform_realizes_comp}
  \lean{Gluck.SpaceForm.spaceFormRealizes_comp}
  \uses{def:spaceform_realizes}
  If $\mathrm{Realizes}\;K\;\gamma\;\mu$ and $\psi$ is $C^1$ with
  $\psi'>0$ everywhere, then
  $\mathrm{Realizes}\;K\;(z\circ\psi)\;(\mu\circ\psi)$.
  ($K$-generic transport of \cref{lem:realizes_spherical_comp};
  un-privatised for the no-rescaling reparametrization step of the $H^2$
  arc-length capstone \cref{thm:arclength_h2_converse}.)
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Chain rule throughout: $(\gamma\circ\psi)'=\psi'\cdot(\gamma'\circ\psi)$ is nonvanishing
  with norm $\psi'\cdot\|\gamma'\circ\psi\|$; the gauge composes to
  $\varphi\circ\psi$; the curvature identity of \cref{def:spaceform_realizes} at
  $\psi(t)$ multiplied by $\psi'(t)>0$ is the identity for $\gamma\circ\psi$ at $t$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\leanok
[space-form converse, positive stage]
  \label{thm:spaceform_converse_pos}
  \lean{Gluck.SpaceForm.spaceFormConverse_pos}
  \uses{def:is_curvature_function, def:four_vertex_condition,
        def:spaceform_realizes, def:is_simple_closed, def:spaceform_four_vertex,
        thm:spaceform_endpoint_winding, lem:spaceform_reconstruction,
        lem:spaceform_circle_realizes, lem:spaceform_realizes_comp,
        lem:reduction_justified_L1}
  Let $K\in\{+1,-1,0\}$ and let $\kappa$ satisfy the $K$-generic
  four-vertex admissibility hypothesis
  $\mathrm{SpaceFormFourVertex}\;K\;\kappa$: $\kappa$ is a curvature
  function (\cref{def:is_curvature_function}: continuous, $2\pi$-periodic,
  strictly positive) satisfying the four-vertex condition
  (\cref{def:four_vertex_condition}), together with the uniform confinement
  floor $\kappa>(1-K)/2$ when $K\le0$ ($\kappa>1$ at
  $K=-1$, $\kappa>1/2$ at $K=0$). Then there is a simple
  closed curve $\gamma$ confined to the open unit disk with
  \[
    \mathrm{IsSimpleClosed}\;\gamma\ \wedge\ \mathrm{Realizes}\;K\;\gamma\;\kappa.
  \]
  At $K=+1$ this recovers \cref{thm:spherical_converse_pos}; at
  $K=-1$ it yields the positive hyperbolic converse
  \cref{thm:hyperbolic_converse_pos}; at $K=0$, dilated out of the
  disk gauge, it yields the second proof of Gluck's Euclidean converse
  (\cref{thm:gluck_converse_spaceForm}).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
  \uses{thm:spaceform_endpoint_winding, lem:spaceform_reconstruction,
        lem:spaceform_circle_realizes, lem:spaceform_realizes_comp,
        lem:exists_c1_circle_inverse, lem:is_simple_closed_comp,
        def:spaceform_flow, lem:reduction_justified_L1,
        cor:positive_curvature_implies_simple, lem:exists_abab_levels,
        def:reconstruct, def:error_vector}
  \leanok
  Split the four-vertex disjunction. \emph{Constant branch:} $\kappa\equiv c$
  with $c>0$ (and $c$ above the threshold $(1-K)/2$ from the floor if
  $K\le0$), so
  \cref{lem:spaceform_circle_realizes} produces the model circle directly.

  \emph{Non-constant branch, flat member $K=0$
  (\texttt{spaceFormConverse\_pos\_nonconst\_flat}):} let $c$ be the
  midpoint of the value-separation window and $r^\ast=1/(2c)<1$ (the floor
  $\kappa>1/2$ gives $c>1/2$); put $s_0=1-r^\ast>0$, pick the step contrast
  $h\le\min(w,\,s_0/8\pi)$ and levels $a=c-h/2$, $b=c+h/2$ inside the
  window (\cref{lem:exists_abab_levels}). The alignment winding
  \cref{lem:reduction_justified_L1} at tolerance $\tau=s_0/2$ returns a $C^1$
  circle reparametrization $g$ with $E(1/(\kappa\circ g))=0$ and a
  two-valued step weight $\sigma\in\{a,b\}$ with
  $\int_0^{2\pi}|1/(\kappa\circ g)-1/\sigma|<s_0/2$. The realized curve is
  the explicit translate $Z=-ir^\ast+\alpha_{\rho}$ of the reconstruction
  curve (\cref{def:reconstruct}) of the halved weight
  $\rho=1/(2(\kappa\circ g))$: it closes because $E(\rho)=E(1/(\kappa\circ
  g))/2=0$, it is simple by \cref{cor:positive_curvature_implies_simple}
  (plus translation invariance), it realizes $\kappa\circ g$ at
  $K=0$ because the flat gauge law is exactly
  $\|Z'\|=1/(2\kappa)$, and it is \emph{confined}: pointwise
  $|1/(2\sigma)-r^\ast|\le h$, so
  $\int_0^{2\pi}|\rho-r^\ast|\le s_0/4+2\pi h\le s_0/2$, and comparing
  $Z$ with the model circle $-ir^\ast e^{i\theta}$ (norm $r^\ast$) gives
  $\|Z\|\le r^\ast+s_0/2<1$ on a period, hence everywhere by periodicity.
  Pull back along the $C^1$ inverse of $g$ as below. No flow, margins, or
  Gr\"onwall transport enter.

  \emph{Non-constant branch, curved members $K=\pm1$
  (\texttt{spaceFormConverse\_pos\_nonconst\_curved}):}
  \cref{thm:spaceform_endpoint_winding} supplies
  $R<1$, $\delta>0$, $h_1$ (with derivative witness $v>0$) and a closed
  admissible trajectory of the truncated flow for $\kappa\circ h_1$; the flow
  specification (\cref{def:spaceform_flow}) converts the flow value into
  $\mathrm{HasDerivWithinAt}$ trajectory hypotheses. The simplicity lemma
  (\texttt{spaceForm\_simplicity}: the trajectory speed is continuous, periodic
  and positive by the admissibility margin, so the periodic extension is a
  reconstruct-curve up to a constant, hence simple) and
  \cref{lem:spaceform_reconstruction} upgrade the periodic extension to a simple
  closed curve realizing $\kappa\circ h_1$. Pull back along the $C^1$ inverse
  $H=h_1^{-1}$ from \cref{lem:exists_c1_circle_inverse} (derivative
  $1/v(H(t))>0$): \cref{lem:spaceform_realizes_comp} transfers realization with
  $(\kappa\circ h_1)\circ H=\kappa$, and \cref{lem:is_simple_closed_comp}
  transfers simplicity.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}\leanok
[mixed-sign space-form four-vertex hypothesis]
  \label{def:mixed_sign_spaceform_four_vertex}
  \lean{Gluck.SpaceForm.MixedSignSpaceFormFourVertex}
  \uses{def:four_vertex_condition, def:centered_radius,
        def:mixed_sign_sphere_four_vertex, def:mixed_sign_hyperbolic_four_vertex,
        def:mixed_sign_four_vertex}
  A function $\kappa:\mathbb R\to\mathbb R$ satisfies the \emph{mixed-sign
  space-form four-vertex hypothesis} at ambient curvature $K$ if it is
  continuous, $2\pi$-periodic, and either
  \begin{itemize}
  \item constant at a level $c$ above the \emph{uniform confinement threshold}
    \[
      \frac{1-K}{2}\;=\;
      \begin{cases}
        0 & K=+1\ (S^2),\\
        1/2 & K=0\ (E^2),\\
        1 & K=-1\ (H^2,\ \text{the escape velocity}),
      \end{cases}
    \]
    the level above which the model circle of curvature $c$ fits in the open
    unit disk ($r^\ast(K,c)<1\iff c>(1-K)/2$,
    \texttt{centeredRadius\_lt\_one\_iff}); or
  \item has value-separated alternating extrema
    $p_1<q_1<p_2<q_2<p_1+2\pi$ ($p_i$ local maxima, $q_i$ local minima) whose
    separation clears the same threshold,
    \[
      \max\Bigl(\tfrac{1-K}{2},\kappa(q_1),\kappa(q_2)\Bigr)
      <\min\bigl(\kappa(p_1),\kappa(p_2)\bigr),
    \]
    together with a window value $c$ in the overlap —
    $\max(\tfrac{1-K}{2},\kappa(q_1),\kappa(q_2))<c<
    \min(\kappa(p_1),\kappa(p_2))$, so in particular $c>(1-K)/2$ and
    the window level is admissible — for which the global confinement floor
    \[
      \kappa(\theta)\;>\;-r^\ast(K,c)
      \qquad\text{for all }\theta
    \]
    is demanded \emph{at positive ambient curvature only} (guard $0<K$),
    $r^\ast$ the centered radius of \cref{def:centered_radius}.
  \end{itemize}
  No global positivity: $\kappa$ may be $\le0$ (at $K=+1$), cross $0$
  (at $K=0$), resp.\ dip into $(-1,0)$ (at $K=-1$) around
  the minima. The floor keeps the position-dependent denominator
  $\kappa-K\langle \gamma,ie^{i\theta}\rangle$ positive along trajectories
  confined to the model radius; its sole consumer is the $S^2$ flow reduction,
  which is why it is guarded to $K>0$ — the $H^2$ arc-length capstone
  \cref{thm:hyperbolic_mixed_converse_exact} carries none, and the flat
  instance reduces to the dilation-free Euclidean
  \cref{thm:dahlberg_converse}, which needs none either. At $K=+1$
  the hypothesis is exactly \cref{def:mixed_sign_sphere_four_vertex}
  (threshold $0$); at $K=-1$ it is
  \cref{def:mixed_sign_hyperbolic_four_vertex} (threshold $1$, floor vacuous);
  at $K=0$ its non-constant branch strengthens the Euclidean
  \cref{def:mixed_sign_four_vertex} (threshold $1/2$ against the
  maxima-positivity clause $0<\min(\kappa(p_1),\kappa(p_2))$).
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}\leanok
[reduction at $K=+1$: to the spherical hypothesis]
  \label{lem:mixed_sign_sphere_of_spaceform}
  \lean{Gluck.SpaceForm.mixedSignSphereFourVertex_of_spaceForm}
  \uses{def:mixed_sign_spaceform_four_vertex, def:mixed_sign_sphere_four_vertex,
        def:centered_radius}
  $\mathrm{MixedSignSpaceFormFourVertex}\;1\;\kappa\Longrightarrow
  \mathrm{MixedSignSphereFourVertex}\;\kappa$
  (\cref{def:mixed_sign_sphere_four_vertex}): at $K=+1$ the space-form
  mixed hypothesis is the spherical one, and the reduction is a near-identity.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  The \texttt{norm\_num} bridge $(1-1)/2=0$ rewrites the threshold in the
  constant branch, the separation and the window membership of $c$ into their
  spherical forms verbatim. The confinement floor (guard $0<1$ discharged by
  \texttt{one\_pos}) is literally the $S^2$ floor:
  $-r^\ast(1,c)=-(\sqrt{c^2+1}-c)$ by \texttt{centeredRadius\_one} (plus
  $1+c^2=c^2+1$).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}\leanok
[reduction at $K=-1$: to the hyperbolic hypothesis]
  \label{lem:mixed_sign_hyperbolic_of_spaceform}
  \lean{Gluck.SpaceForm.mixedSignHyperbolicFourVertex_of_spaceForm}
  \uses{def:mixed_sign_spaceform_four_vertex,
        def:mixed_sign_hyperbolic_four_vertex}
  $\mathrm{MixedSignSpaceFormFourVertex}\;(-1)\;\kappa\Longrightarrow
  \mathrm{MixedSignHyperbolicFourVertex}\;\kappa$
  (\cref{def:mixed_sign_hyperbolic_four_vertex}): at $K=-1$ the
  space-form mixed hypothesis is the hyperbolic one.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  \leanok

  hyperbolic escape velocity: the four-vertex package (ordering, extrema,
  separation $\max(1,\cdot)<\min(\cdot)$, window value $c$) is then identical,
  the hyperbolic window clause $1<c$ follows from $1\le\max(1,\cdot)<c$, and
  the guarded confinement floor is vacuous at $K=-1$ — the
  hyperbolic arc-length route needs none.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\leanok
[space-form converse, mixed sign]
  \label{thm:spaceform_mixed_converse}
  \lean{Gluck.SpaceForm.spaceFormMixedConverse}
  \uses{def:mixed_sign_spaceform_four_vertex,
        lem:mixed_sign_sphere_of_spaceform,
        lem:mixed_sign_hyperbolic_of_spaceform,
        lem:realizes_one_iff_spherical,
        def:mixed_sign_sphere_four_vertex, thm:spherical_converse,
        def:mixed_sign_hyperbolic_four_vertex,
        thm:hyperbolic_mixed_converse_exact,
        def:is_simple_closed, def:spaceform_realizes}
  Let $K\in\{+1,-1\}$. If $\kappa$ satisfies the mixed-sign space-form
  four-vertex hypothesis (\cref{def:mixed_sign_spaceform_four_vertex}), there is
  a simple closed curve confined to the open disk realizing $\kappa$ exactly as
  its space-form geodesic curvature:
  \[
    \mathrm{MixedSignSpaceFormFourVertex}\;K\;\kappa
    \;\Longrightarrow\;
    \exists\,\gamma,\ \mathrm{IsSimpleClosed}\;\gamma\ \wedge\
    \mathrm{Realizes}\;K\;\gamma\;\kappa.
  \]
  Subsumes \cref{thm:spaceform_converse_pos} at the curved signs, and unifies
  the spherical and hyperbolic Dahlberg converses in one $K$-generic
  statement.
  \emph{Honest accounting:} this theorem is proved by \textbf{reduction to the
  two completed per-geometry capstones} — \cref{thm:spherical_converse} at
  $K=+1$ and \cref{thm:hyperbolic_mixed_converse_exact} at
  $K=-1$ — not by an independent $K$-generic mixed
  construction. The two-way sign hypothesis is genuine even though
  \cref{def:mixed_sign_spaceform_four_vertex} makes sense at $K=0$:
  the confined flat statement is false in general (scale rigidity — see the
  paragraph after \cref{thm:hyperbolic_dahlberg_converse}), and the flat member
  of the mixed table is the dilation-free
  \cref{thm:spaceform_mixed_converse_flat}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
  \uses{lem:mixed_sign_sphere_of_spaceform,
        lem:mixed_sign_hyperbolic_of_spaceform,
        lem:realizes_one_iff_spherical,
        thm:spherical_converse, thm:hyperbolic_mixed_converse_exact}
  \leanok
  Case on $K$. At $K=+1$:
  \cref{lem:mixed_sign_sphere_of_spaceform} strengthens the hypothesis to
  \cref{def:mixed_sign_sphere_four_vertex}, the spherical mixed capstone
  \cref{thm:spherical_converse} produces a simple closed spherical realization,
  and \cref{lem:realizes_one_iff_spherical} converts
  $\mathrm{RealizesSphericalCurvature}$ into $\mathrm{Realizes}\;1$. At
  $K=-1$: \cref{lem:mixed_sign_hyperbolic_of_spaceform} strengthens
  the hypothesis to \cref{def:mixed_sign_hyperbolic_four_vertex} (the floor is
  dropped), and the exact-profile hyperbolic capstone
  \cref{thm:hyperbolic_mixed_converse_exact} — whose conclusion is already
  stated in terms of $\mathrm{Realizes}\;(-1)$ — applies directly.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\leanok

  \label{thm:hyperbolic_dahlberg_converse}

  as the geodesic curvature of a simple closed curve in the hyperbolic plane.
  This is the converse of the four vertex theorem in $H^2$ (Dahlberg 2005,
  \emph{Converse of the four vertex theorem}, Proc.\ AMS 133, hyperbolic
  transport).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
  \uses{thm:spaceform_mixed_converse}
  \leanok
  Specialize \cref{thm:spaceform_mixed_converse} at $K=-1$ (the sign
  disjunction is discharged by \texttt{Or.inr rfl}).
\end{proof}

\paragraph{The flat member and scale rigidity.} Why does
\cref{thm:spaceform_mixed_converse} keep the two-way sign hypothesis
$K\in\{+1,-1\}$ when \cref{def:mixed_sign_spaceform_four_vertex}
makes sense at $K=0$? Its conclusion
$\mathrm{Realizes}\;K$ is \emph{confined}: it contains$\|\gamma(t)\|<1$. At $K=\pm1$ the open disk is the geometry itself (the
conformal model of $S^2$ resp.\ $H^2$), but at $K=0$ confinement is
a genuine restriction: $\mathrm{Realizes}\;0$ pins the scale of the curve — a
dilation multiplies the realized Euclidean curvature by the reciprocal factor,
so there is no rescaling freedom inside the predicate — and with unrestricted
minima a realizing curve can be forced arbitrarily large, so the confined flat
statement is false in general. (Contrast the positive stage: there the global
floor $\kappa>1/2$ bounds the whole profile, and the flat branch of
\cref{thm:spaceform_converse_pos} squeezes the curve against the model circle
of radius $1/(2c)<1$; the mixed hypothesis bounds only the maxima.) The flat
member of the mixed table therefore concludes in the dilation-free Euclidean
predicate (\cref{thm:spaceform_mixed_converse_flat} below); a confined flat
version under the window floor $\kappa>-r^\ast(0,c)$ would need the flat
$L^1$-squeeze construction of the positive stage transported to mixed sign
(the flat fork-A port) — future work. With this reading the uniform threshold
row $(1-K)/2=(0,\,1/2,\,1)$ of the positive stage now covers the
mixed table too: sphere \cref{thm:spherical_converse}, plane
\cref{thm:spaceform_mixed_converse_flat}, hyperbolic plane
\cref{thm:hyperbolic_dahlberg_converse}.

\begin{lemma}\leanok
[reduction at $K=0$: to the Euclidean mixed-sign hypothesis]
  \label{lem:mixed_sign_four_vertex_of_spaceform_flat}
  \lean{Gluck.SpaceForm.mixedSignFourVertex_of_spaceForm_flat}
  \uses{def:mixed_sign_spaceform_four_vertex, def:mixed_sign_four_vertex}
  If $\kappa$ satisfies $\mathrm{MixedSignSpaceFormFourVertex}\;0\;\kappa$ and
  is non-constant, it satisfies the Euclidean mixed-sign hypothesis
  $\mathrm{MixedSignFourVertex}$ (\cref{def:mixed_sign_four_vertex}) of the
  Dahlberg converse.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Non-constancy refutes the constant branch. In the four-vertex branch the
  package transfers verbatim: the separation
  $\max(\kappa(q_1),\kappa(q_2))<\min(\kappa(p_1),\kappa(p_2))$ weakens from
  the thresholded
  $\max(\tfrac12,\kappa(q_1),\kappa(q_2))<\min(\kappa(p_1),\kappa(p_2))$, and
  the maxima-positivity clause $0<\min(\kappa(p_1),\kappa(p_2))$ follows from
  $0<\tfrac12\le\max(\tfrac12,\cdot)<\min(\cdot)$. The guarded floor is
  vacuous at $K=0$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\leanok
[space-form mixed converse, flat member]
  \label{thm:spaceform_mixed_converse_flat}
  \lean{Gluck.SpaceForm.spaceFormMixedConverse_flat}
  \uses{def:mixed_sign_spaceform_four_vertex, thm:dahlberg_converse,
        lem:mixed_sign_four_vertex_of_spaceform_flat, thm:gluck_converse,
        def:is_simple_closed, def:realizes_curvature}
  A profile satisfying the mixed-sign space-form hypothesis at $K=0$
  (threshold $1/2$) is realized as the Euclidean curvature of a simple closed
  curve, in the \emph{dilation-free} predicate:
  \[
    \mathrm{MixedSignSpaceFormFourVertex}\;0\;\kappa
    \;\Longrightarrow\;
    \exists\,\gamma,\ \mathrm{IsSimpleClosed}\;\gamma\ \wedge\
    \mathrm{RealizesCurvature}\;\gamma\;\kappa.
  \]
  The conclusion is deliberately \emph{not} the confined $\mathrm{Realizes}\;0$
  — see the scale-rigidity paragraph above.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
  \uses{lem:mixed_sign_four_vertex_of_spaceform_flat, thm:dahlberg_converse,
        thm:gluck_converse}
  \leanok
  Constant branch: $\kappa\equiv c$ with $c>1/2>0$, so the round circle of
  radius $1/c$ realizes it (the constant case of \cref{thm:gluck_converse}).
  Non-constant branch: the strict value separation makes $\kappa$ non-constant
  (\texttt{not\_constant\_of\_separation}), so
  \cref{lem:mixed_sign_four_vertex_of_spaceform_flat} strengthens the
  hypothesis to \cref{def:mixed_sign_four_vertex} and the Euclidean Dahlberg
  converse \cref{thm:dahlberg_converse} applies.
\end{proof}

\section{Two proofs of Gluck's theorem}
\label{sec:two_proofs}

The flat member $K=0$ of \cref{thm:spaceform_converse_pos} lives in
the open unit disk with the conformal gauge $\lambda=2$, so its realization
predicate is the Euclidean one up to a factor $2$ and a confinement clause.
Undoing both by a dilation — the symmetry of $E^2$ that $S^2$ and $H^2$ lack —
produces a \emph{second, flow-route proof} of Gluck's converse to the
four-vertex theorem, with statement identical to the 1971-route capstone
\cref{thm:gluck_converse}.

\begin{lemma}\leanok
[the flat space form is the Euclidean plane]
  \label{lem:realizes_curvature_of_realizes_zero}
  \lean{Gluck.realizesCurvature_of_realizes_zero}
  \uses{def:spaceform_realizes, def:realizes_curvature}
  If $\mathrm{Realizes}\;0\;\gamma\;\kappa$ (\cref{def:spaceform_realizes}) then
  $\mathrm{RealizesCurvature}\;\gamma\;(2\kappa)$
  (\cref{def:realizes_curvature}): at $K=0$ the space-form gauge law
  $\tfrac{1+0\cdot\|\gamma\|^2}{2}\varphi'=(\kappa-0)\|\gamma'\|$ is exactly
  $\varphi'=(2\kappa)\|\gamma'\|$. The factor $2$ is the flat conformal gauge
  $\lambda=2$ of the disk model; the confinement clause $\|\gamma\|<1$ is simply
  dropped.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Unfold both predicates; the $C^1$, regularity and tangent-angle clauses
  coincide, and the curvature identity is the space-form one multiplied by
  $2$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\leanok
[Gluck's converse, the flow proof]
  \label{thm:gluck_converse_spaceForm}
  \lean{Gluck.gluck_converse_spaceForm}
  \uses{def:is_curvature_function, def:four_vertex_condition,
        def:is_simple_closed, def:realizes_curvature,
        thm:spaceform_converse_pos, lem:realizes_curvature_of_realizes_zero,
        lem:realizes_curvature_smul, lem:issimpleclosed_smul,
        lem:sphere_curvature_lower_bound, def:spaceform_four_vertex}
  Let $\kappa$ be a curvature function satisfying the four-vertex condition.
  Then there is a simple closed curve $\gamma$ with
  $\mathrm{RealizesCurvature}\;\gamma\;\kappa$ — the statement of
  \cref{thm:gluck_converse}, proved by the space-form flow route at
  $K=0$ plus a dilation.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
  \uses{thm:spaceform_converse_pos, lem:realizes_curvature_of_realizes_zero,
        lem:realizes_curvature_smul, lem:issimpleclosed_smul,
        lem:sphere_curvature_lower_bound}
  \leanok
  The dilation argument. Let $m>0$ be a uniform lower bound for $\kappa$
  (\cref{lem:sphere_curvature_lower_bound}) and set $\mu=\kappa/m$, a
  curvature function with $\mu>1>1/2$ pointwise whose four-vertex data
  transport along the positive scaling (extrema and strict value separation
  are preserved by $x\mapsto x/m$). Thus
  $\mathrm{SpaceFormFourVertex}\;0\;\mu$ holds and
  \cref{thm:spaceform_converse_pos} at $K=0$ yields a simple closed $w$ in the
  open unit disk with $\mathrm{Realizes}\;0\;w\;\mu$; by
  \cref{lem:realizes_curvature_of_realizes_zero}, $w$ realizes the Euclidean
  curvature $2\mu=(2/m)\kappa$. Dilating by $2/m$ — curvature scales by the
  reciprocal (\cref{lem:realizes_curvature_smul}), simplicity is preserved
  (\cref{lem:issimpleclosed_smul}) — the curve $\gamma=(2/m)\,w$ realizes
  $\bigl((2/m)\kappa\bigr)\big/(2/m)=\kappa$.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Comparison of the two proofs.} Both capstones prove the same
statement, from the same hypotheses, by genuinely different routes.

\emph{The 1971/classical route} (\cref{chap:reduction},
\cref{chap:fourvertex}; \cref{thm:gluck_converse}) works in the tangent-angle
parametrization with radius of curvature $\rho=1/\kappa$ and rests on the
\emph{linear structure of the closure conditions}: a curve closes iff
$\oint\rho e^{i\theta}\,d\theta=0$, an $L^1$-continuous linear functional of
the profile. Its heart is an alignment argument: a two-parameter family of
breakpoint-aligning reparametrizations, the explicit bicircle error map on the
configuration square, and the winding number $-1$ of that error map
(\cref{lem:reduction_justified}). It needs the exact-profile alignment of the
four-vertex data with the reference bicircle, and its degree argument runs
over a \emph{configuration} space.

\emph{The flow route} (\cref{chap:spaceform_core}, this chapter;
\cref{thm:gluck_converse_spaceForm}) reconstructs the curve by an ODE in the
conformal disk model, uniformly in the ambient curvature $K$:
truncation and the Picard--Lindel\"of flow, step-model margins and Gr\"onwall
$L^1$-transport, and the first-variation endpoint winding over the
\emph{start point} of the flow. Confinement is intrinsic, coming from the
curvature lower bound (the model circle of level $c$ has coordinate radius
$1/(2c)$ — the curvature floor bounds the curve's size), and no alignment of
the profile with a reference shape is required at the curved signs. What it
needs that the classical route does not is the dilation post-processing to
remove the disk confinement — a symmetry available only in $E^2$, which is
exactly why the $H^2$ converse was hard: there no dilation can trade a
stronger floor for confinement, and the escape-velocity threshold $\kappa>1$
is genuinely part of the statement.

At the crossing point $K=0$ the two routes touch: the flat gauge
field is position-independent, the endpoint winding degenerates
($\eta(0)=0$), and the flat branch of \cref{thm:spaceform_converse_pos}
borrows the classical alignment winding for \emph{closure}
(\cref{lem:reduction_justified_L1}) while keeping the space-form
reconstruction, simplicity and confinement analysis. The second proof is thus
independent of the first in every ingredient except the closure degree
argument, which is shared infrastructure — and it adds what the first proof
cannot state: the same engine, at $K=\pm1$, proves the spherical
and hyperbolic converses with no change to the argument.
