\section{Overview}

Fix $n \ge 3$ and a positive profile $\kappa : \ZMod n \to \mathbb{R}$,
$\kappa_i > 0$. Recall (\cref{chap:discrete_defs}) that for $K = 0$
\[
  \mathrm{turningAngle}\,\kappa\,\ell\,i
    \;=\; \arcsin\!\Big(\tfrac{\kappa_i\,\ell_{i-1}}{2}\Big)
        + \arcsin\!\Big(\tfrac{\kappa_i\,\ell_i}{2}\Big),
  \qquad
  \mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,\ell \;=\; \sum_{i \in \ZMod n}
    \mathrm{turningAngle}\,\kappa\,\ell\,i,
\]
and that $\mathrm{closureGap}\,\kappa\,\ell \in \mathbb{C}$ is the position
residual of the development, so that $\mathrm{RealizesR2}\,\kappa$ asks for a
moderate-arc $\ell$ with $\mathrm{closureGap} = 0$,
$\mathrm{turningSum} = 2\pi$, and a simple development. In the positive case
simplicity is free by L1 (\cref{thm:convex_simple}), so the task reduces to
solving the two closing equations
\[
  \mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,\ell = 2\pi
  \qquad\text{and}\qquad
  \mathrm{closureGap}\,\kappa\,\ell = 0 \in \mathbb{R}^2 .
\]
These are three scalar equations in the $n$ edge lengths ($n \ge 4$, so the
solution set is generically $(n-3)$-dimensional and nonempty; $n = 3$ is triangle
rigidity, \cref{chap:discrete_budget} N0). Mirroring Gluck's smooth positive
proof (deturck-gluck §III--IV), the position closure $\mathrm{closureGap} = 0$ is
a \emph{winding / degree} statement, not a coordinate-wise sign condition. The
engine (\cref{sec:closure}) fixes the turning via the single-edge tune
(\cref{sec:edge_tune}) and produces a zero of $\mathrm{closureGap}$ on the turning
manifold by a degree argument \textbf{anchored at the centrally-symmetric case},
which closes for free. A generic $2$-edge slice does \emph{not} carry the winding
certificate (\cref{rem:generic_slice_fails}); the certificate lives on a
symmetry-anchored deformation (\cref{sec:closure}).

\begin{remark}[why not solve turning first]
  \label{rem:no_ray_retraction}
  An earlier route (iter-065) tried to solve $\mathrm{turningSum} = 2\pi$
  \emph{first}, by a monotone rescaling retraction along a ray $t \cdot \ell^0$
  of edge lengths (\cref{sec:umlauf}), and then position on the resulting
  $2$-dimensional slice. This is \emph{not} viable: a numeric probe
  (\texttt{references/verify\_umlauf\_capacity.py}) shows that for a non-constant
  positive $\kappa$ \emph{no} ray reaches $\mathrm{turningSum} = 2\pi$ before the
  moderate-arc wall --- even the numerically optimized best base $\ell^0$ falls
  short (worst capacity $3.2$--$5.0 < 2\pi$ across $n$; $\mathrm{DFV}$ profiles
  included). Algebraically, making all $2n$ arcsin arguments
  $\kappa_i\,t\,\ell^0_{i-1}/2,\ \kappa_i\,t\,\ell^0_i/2$ reach the wall at a
  common $t$ forces $\kappa_i\ell^0_{i-1} = \kappa_i\ell^0_i$ for all $i$, i.e.\
  $\ell^0$ constant \emph{and} $\kappa$ constant; otherwise the ray saturates at
  the first wall with $\sup_t \mathrm{turningSum}(\kappa, t\cdot\ell^0) < 2\pi$.
  The retraction of \cref{sec:umlauf} is therefore off the critical path (its
  ceiling hypothesis is not generally satisfiable); it survives only as
  conditional infrastructure. The joint $\mathbb{R}^3$ solve avoids the ray
  entirely, and the probe confirms the full moderate domain contains
  $\mathrm{turningSum} \ge 2\pi$ robustly for $n \ge 4$.
\end{remark}

\section{The Umlauf rescaling retraction}
\label{sec:umlauf}

\emph{Status (corrected @066).} The three lemmas of this section are FORMALIZED
and axiom-clean, but they are now \textbf{off the critical path} for the closing
engine: they establish existence and uniqueness of a scale $t^\star$ solving
$\mathrm{turningSum}(\kappa, t^\star\cdot\ell^0) = 2\pi$ \emph{under a ceiling
hypothesis} that (\cref{rem:no_ray_retraction}) cannot be met by a ray for a
general positive $\kappa$. They are retained as conditional infrastructure
(useful on the restricted class where a balanced ray exists); the engine of
\cref{sec:closure} does not depend on them.

For a fixed positive base $\ell^0 : \ZMod n \to \mathbb{R}$ ($\ell^0_i > 0$) and
a scalar $t > 0$ write $t \cdot \ell^0$ for the pointwise scaling. Each turning
angle
\[
  \mathrm{turningAngle}\,\kappa\,(t\cdot\ell^0)\,i
    = \arcsin\!\Big(\tfrac{\kappa_i\,t\,\ell^0_{i-1}}{2}\Big)
    + \arcsin\!\Big(\tfrac{\kappa_i\,t\,\ell^0_i}{2}\Big)
\]
has both arguments positive (as $\kappa_i, \ell^0_j, t > 0$) and strictly
increasing in $t$; since $\arcsin$ is strictly increasing on $[-1,1]$
(\texttt{strictMonoOn\_arcsin}), each turning angle — and hence the total — is
strictly increasing in $t$ on the range of $t$ for which $t\cdot\ell^0$ stays in
the moderate-arc domain.

\begin{lemma}[turning sum is strictly increasing under scaling]
  \label{lem:turning_scale_mono}
  \lean{Gluck.Discrete.turningSum_smul_lt}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:discrete_development, def:moderate_arc}
  Let $\kappa_i > 0$ and $\ell^0_i > 0$ for all $i$, and let $0 < s < t$ with
  $\mathrm{ModerateArc}\,0\,\kappa\,(t\cdot\ell^0)$. Then
  \[
    \mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,(s\cdot\ell^0)
      \;<\; \mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,(t\cdot\ell^0).
  \]
  (Moderate-arcness at the larger scale $t$ implies it at the smaller scale $s$,
  since every $\arcsin$ argument shrinks.)
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:moderate_arc}
  It suffices to compare termwise. For each $i$, both
  $\kappa_i s \ell^0_{i-1}/2 < \kappa_i t \ell^0_{i-1}/2$ and
  $\kappa_i s \ell^0_i/2 < \kappa_i t \ell^0_i/2$ hold ($\kappa_i,\ell^0_j>0$,
  $s<t$), and each right-hand argument lies in $(-1,1)$ by
  $\mathrm{ModerateArc}$ at scale $t$ (\texttt{wall\_left}/\texttt{wall\_right}),
  so each left-hand argument lies in $[-1,1)$ too. As $\arcsin$ is strictly
  increasing on $[-1,1]$, each summand strictly increases, and a finite sum of
  termwise-strict increases is strictly larger (\texttt{Finset.sum\_lt\_sum} with
  one strict term suffices; here every term is strict).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[turning sum vanishes in the small-scale limit]
  \label{lem:turning_scale_zero}
  \lean{Gluck.Discrete.turningSum_smul_tendsto_zero}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:discrete_development}
  For $\kappa$, $\ell^0$ as above the function
  $t \mapsto \mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,(t\cdot\ell^0)$ is continuous and tends
  to $0$ as $t \to 0^+$ (indeed it equals $0$ at $t = 0$).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Each summand is a finite sum of $\arcsin$ of a continuous (linear in $t$)
  argument; $\arcsin$ is continuous on $[-1,1]$ (\texttt{continuous\_arcsin}),
  and at $t = 0$ every argument is $0$ with $\arcsin 0 = 0$
  (\texttt{Real.arcsin\_zero}), so the total is $0$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Umlauf slice exists for a balanced base]
  \label{lem:exists_umlauf_scale}
  \lean{Gluck.Discrete.exists_umlauf_scale}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:turning_scale_mono, lem:turning_scale_zero, def:moderate_arc}
  Let $n \ge 3$, $\kappa_i > 0$ and $\ell^0_i > 0$ for all $i$, and suppose there
  is a \emph{ceiling} $t_1 > 0$ with
  \[
    \mathrm{ModerateArc}\,0\,\kappa\,(t_1\cdot\ell^0)
    \qquad\text{and}\qquad
    2\pi \;\le\; \mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,(t_1\cdot\ell^0).
  \]
  Then there is $t^\star \in (0, t_1]$ with
  $\mathrm{ModerateArc}\,0\,\kappa\,(t^\star\cdot\ell^0)$ and
  $\mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,(t^\star\cdot\ell^0) = 2\pi$
  (uniqueness: \cref{lem:umlauf_scale_unique}).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:turning_scale_mono, lem:turning_scale_zero}
  On $(0, t_1]$ the map $g(t) = \mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,(t\cdot\ell^0)$ is
  continuous (\cref{lem:turning_scale_zero}) and, by
  \cref{lem:turning_scale_mono}, strictly increasing, with
  $g(t) \to 0 < 2\pi$ as $t \to 0^+$ and $g(t_1) \ge 2\pi$. The intermediate
  value theorem on the interval — Mathlib
  \texttt{intermediate\_value\_Ioc} / \texttt{intermediate\_value\_Icc} applied
  to the continuous $g$, or \texttt{exists\_Ioo\_of\_tendsto} against the
  $t\to0^+$ limit — yields $t^\star \in (0, t_1]$ with $g(t^\star) = 2\pi$;
  uniqueness is immediate from strict monotonicity. Moderate-arcness at
  $t^\star \le t_1$ follows from moderate-arcness at $t_1$ (arguments only
  shrink), exactly as in \cref{lem:turning_scale_mono}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[why the ceiling hypothesis is unavoidable — and generally unmet]
  Without a ceiling the retraction can \emph{fail}: for a base $\ell^0$ with one
  edge much longer than the rest, the long edge's $\arcsin$ argument reaches the
  moderate-arc wall while the others are still small, so
  $\sup_t \mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,(t\cdot\ell^0) < 2\pi$ and no admissible
  $t$ gives turning $2\pi$. This corrects the naive ``range $= (0, n\pi)$'' claim
  of discrete\_plan.md §5.1. \textbf{Moreover (numeric probe @066,
  \cref{rem:no_ray_retraction}), the ceiling cannot be met by ANY ray for a
  general positive $\kappa$}: the balanced base is not constructible, so the
  retraction is off the critical path and the engine proceeds by the
  symmetry-anchored degree argument of \cref{sec:closure}.
\end{remark}

\begin{lemma}[uniqueness of the Umlauf scale]
  \label{lem:umlauf_scale_unique}
  \lean{Gluck.Discrete.umlauf_scale_unique}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:turning_scale_mono}
  With $\kappa$, $\ell^0$ as in \cref{lem:exists_umlauf_scale}, at most one
  $t \in (0, t_1]$ with $\mathrm{ModerateArc}\,0\,\kappa\,(t\cdot\ell^0)$ has
  $\mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,(t\cdot\ell^0) = 2\pi$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:turning_scale_mono}
  If two distinct scales $s < t$ both solved the equation, strict monotonicity
  (\cref{lem:turning_scale_mono}) would force
  $\mathrm{turningSum}(\kappa,s\cdot\ell^0) < \mathrm{turningSum}(\kappa,t\cdot\ell^0)$,
  contradicting both equal to $2\pi$; \texttt{lt\_trichotomy}.
\end{proof}

\section{The balanced base --- RETIRED (numerically falsified)}
\label{sec:balanced}

\emph{This section is retired.} The iter-065 architecture asked for a base
$\ell^0$ (a ray in edge-length space) whose turning capacity exceeds $2\pi$
before the moderate-arc wall, so as to instantiate the ceiling hypothesis of
\cref{lem:exists_umlauf_scale}. The proposed construction ($\ell^0_i \propto
1/\kappa_i$, ``all $\arcsin$ arguments reach $1$ simultaneously'') is
\textbf{false}: with $\ell^0_i = 1/\kappa_i$ the right argument at vertex $i$ is
$\kappa_i t \ell^0_i/2 = t/2$, but the left argument is $\kappa_i t \ell^0_{i-1}/2

$\kappa$ is constant. The numeric probe
(\texttt{references/verify\_umlauf\_capacity.py}) confirms no ray works even after
optimizing $\ell^0$ (\cref{rem:no_ray_retraction}). There is no balanced base to
construct; the turning coordinate is handled inside the joint solve of
\cref{sec:closure} instead. Retained only as a record of the dead end.

\section{Single-edge turning tune (route-shared inner solve)}
\label{sec:edge_tune}

Unlike the \emph{ray} retraction of \cref{sec:umlauf} (which scales \emph{all}
edges together and is obstructed, \cref{rem:no_ray_retraction}), the turning sum
is monotone in \emph{one} edge with the others held fixed, and this single-edge
dependence is not obstructed: it is the discrete
$\partial\theta/\partial\ell_k > 0$ fact. It is needed by \emph{both} candidate
closing engines (\cref{sec:closure}): the turning face of the joint
$\mathbb{R}^3$ Poincar\'e--Miranda box, and the inner tune of the $2$-D fallback.
We record it here to atomic, formalizable detail.

The edge value $\ell_k$ enters $\mathrm{turningAngle}\,0\,\kappa\,\ell\,i$ at
exactly two vertices: at $i = k$ through the right summand
$\arcsin(\kappa_k\,\ell_k/2)$, and at $i = k+1$ through the left summand
$\arcsin(\kappa_{k+1}\,\ell_k/2)$ (recall $\mathrm{turningAngle}$ at vertex $i$
uses the edges $\ell_{i-1}$ and $\ell_i$). For $n \ge 3$ the two vertices $k$ and
$k+1$ are distinct, and every other vertex's turning angle is independent of
$\ell_k$. Write $\mathrm{update}\,\ell\,k\,a$ for the base $\ell$ with its
$k$-th edge reset to $a$ (Mathlib \texttt{Function.update}).

\begin{lemma}\leanok
[turning sum strictly increases in a single edge]
  \label{lem:turningSum_edge_mono}
  \lean{Gluck.Discrete.turningSum_update_lt}
  \uses{def:discrete_development, def:moderate_arc}
  Let $n \ge 3$, $[\mathrm{NeZero}\,n]$, $\kappa_i > 0$ for all $i$, let
  $\ell : \ZMod n \to \mathbb{R}$ be a base, fix $k \in \ZMod n$, and let
  $0 < a < b$ with $\mathrm{ModerateArc}\,0\,\kappa\,(\mathrm{update}\,\ell\,k\,b)$.
  Then
  \[
    \mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,(\mathrm{update}\,\ell\,k\,a)
      \;<\; \mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,(\mathrm{update}\,\ell\,k\,b).
  \]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  \uses{def:moderate_arc}
  \leanok
  Compare the two turning sums termwise over $\ZMod n$. For a vertex $i \notin
  \{k,\,k+1\}$ both summands of $\mathrm{turningAngle}\,0\,\kappa\,\cdot\,i$ use
  edges other than $\ell_k$, so the term is unchanged by the update
  ($\mathrm{Function.update}$ leaves those coordinates fixed). At $i = k$ only the
  right summand $\arcsin(\kappa_k\,(\cdot)_k/2)$ changes, and at $i = k+1$ only the
  left summand $\arcsin(\kappa_{k+1}\,(\cdot)_k/2)$ changes; $k \ne k+1$ since
  $n \ge 3$. In each changed summand the argument scales with the edge value:
  $\kappa_k\,a/2 < \kappa_k\,b/2$ and $\kappa_{k+1}\,a/2 < \kappa_{k+1}\,b/2$
  (both $\kappa > 0$, $a < b$). The larger argument lies in $(-1,1)$ by the wall
  at $b$ (\texttt{wall\_right} at vertex $k$, \texttt{wall\_left} at vertex
  $k+1$), so the smaller lies in $[-1,1)$ too, and $\arcsin$ is strictly
  increasing on $[-1,1]$ (\texttt{strictMonoOn\_arcsin}); each changed summand
  strictly increases. Hence, termwise, every vertex's turning angle is $\le$ the
  updated one and strictly $<$ at $i = k$. \texttt{Finset.sum\_lt\_sum} (over
  $\mathrm{Finset.univ}$, with the $\le$ everywhere and the one strict term at
  $k$) gives the strict inequality of the sums.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}\leanok
[single-edge turning tune]
  \label{lem:exists_edge_turning_scale}
  \lean{Gluck.Discrete.exists_edge_turning_scale}
  \uses{lem:turningSum_edge_mono, def:moderate_arc}
  Let $n \ge 3$, $[\mathrm{NeZero}\,n]$, $\kappa_i > 0$ for all $i$, let $\ell$ be
  a base, fix $k \in \ZMod n$, and let $0 < \mathrm{lo} < \mathrm{hi}$ with
  \[
    \mathrm{ModerateArc}\,0\,\kappa\,(\mathrm{update}\,\ell\,k\,\mathrm{hi}),
    \quad
    \mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,(\mathrm{update}\,\ell\,k\,\mathrm{lo}) < 2\pi
    \le \mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,(\mathrm{update}\,\ell\,k\,\mathrm{hi}).
  \]
  Then there is $a \in (\mathrm{lo}, \mathrm{hi}]$ with
  $\mathrm{ModerateArc}\,0\,\kappa\,(\mathrm{update}\,\ell\,k\,a)$ and
  $\mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,(\mathrm{update}\,\ell\,k\,a) = 2\pi$; it is
  unique in $(\mathrm{lo},\mathrm{hi}]$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  \uses{lem:turningSum_edge_mono}
  \leanok
  Let $g(a) = \mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,(\mathrm{update}\,\ell\,k\,a)$. Only the
  two summands at vertices $k$, $k+1$ depend on $a$, each an $\arcsin$ of a
  function linear in $a$; $\arcsin$ is continuous on $[-1,1]$
  (\texttt{continuous\_arcsin}), and on $[\mathrm{lo},\mathrm{hi}]$ every argument
  stays in $[-1,1)$ by the wall at $\mathrm{hi}$, so $g$ is continuous on
  $[\mathrm{lo},\mathrm{hi}]$. Since $g(\mathrm{lo}) < 2\pi \le g(\mathrm{hi})$,
  the intermediate value theorem (\texttt{intermediate\_value\_Icc}) yields
  $a \in [\mathrm{lo},\mathrm{hi}]$ with $g(a) = 2\pi$; as $g(\mathrm{lo}) < 2\pi$
  we have $a > \mathrm{lo}$. Moderate-arcness at $a \le \mathrm{hi}$ follows from
  moderate-arcness at $\mathrm{hi}$ (the $\arcsin$ arguments at $k$, $k+1$ only
  shrink, and the other vertices are untouched). Uniqueness is immediate from the
  strict monotonicity of \cref{lem:turningSum_edge_mono}.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}\leanok
[uniqueness of the single-edge turning scale]
  \label{lem:edge_turning_scale_unique}
  \lean{Gluck.Discrete.edge_turning_scale_unique}
  \uses{lem:turningSum_edge_mono}
  Let $n \ge 3$, $[\mathrm{NeZero}\,n]$, $\kappa_i > 0$, fix $k \in \ZMod n$ and a
  base $\ell$. If $a_1, a_2 > 0$ both satisfy
  $\mathrm{ModerateArc}\,0\,\kappa\,(\mathrm{update}\,\ell\,k\,a_j)$ and
  $\mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,(\mathrm{update}\,\ell\,k\,a_j) = 2\pi$, then
  $a_1 = a_2$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \uses{lem:turningSum_edge_mono}
  \leanok
  If $a_1 \ne a_2$, say $a_1 < a_2$, then $\mathrm{turningSum}$ at the smaller is
  strictly less than at the larger by \cref{lem:turningSum_edge_mono}, so the two
  turning sums cannot both equal $2\pi$; the trichotomy on $a_1, a_2$ gives
  $a_1 = a_2$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[a generic $2$-edge slice carries no winding]

  The turning tune (\cref{sec:edge_tune}) fixes one edge $\ell_k$ so that
  $\mathrm{turningSum} = 2\pi$, leaving the closure map
  $G(\ell_p,\ell_q) = \mathrm{closureGap}\,\kappa\,\ell$ on a $2$-parameter slice
  (the other $n-3$ edges pinned at a base). The iter-068 idea was to wind $G$
  along the boundary of the inner-feasible region. This is \emph{not} a valid
  certificate: the degree probe \texttt{references/verify\_2d\_dR\_winding.py}
  computes the degree of $G$ over the actual inner-feasible region (via a
  $2\times 2$-cell winding sum, so interior edges telescope to the $\partial R$
  integral) and finds it is $0$ in the \emph{majority} of random positive
  profiles, $\pm 1$ only in a minority. A generic slice is not the winding-$\pm 1$
  loop: most positive profiles are not even realizable
  (\cref{rem:dfv_not_necessary} --- $\mathrm{DFV}$ is sufficient but not
  necessary, yet ``most'' random profiles fail it), and even for a realizable
  $\kappa$ a random slice may miss the $(n-3)$-dimensional solution set. Following
  deturck-gluck §III--IV, the winding-$\pm 1$ loop is instead the \emph{bicircle
  configuration} loop (Prop~15.1--15.2), anchored at the symmetric ``core''
  (Prop~9.1: closure iff opposite arcs equal).
\end{remark}

\subsection*{Route A (primary): central-symmetry continuation}

The best-de-risked engine anchors the degree argument at the centrally-symmetric
case, which closes for free.

This is the discrete analogue of the DeTurck--Gluck ``opposite arcs equal
$\Rightarrow$ closes'' criterion (Prop~9.1), and it is \emph{route-independent}:
it is the $t=0$ anchor of Route~A and the symmetric ``core'' of Route~B. It is
crux-free and provable now; it is the committed prover target of this section.
We phrase central symmetry as \emph{half-period} $m = n/2$: $n = 2m$ and both
$\kappa$ and $\ell$ satisfy $x_{i+m} = x_i$ (indices in $\ZMod n$, so $m$ is the
class $(m : \ZMod n)$).

\begin{lemma}\leanok
[central symmetry closes]
  \label{lem:central_symmetry_closes}
  \lean{Gluck.Discrete.central_symmetry_closes}
  \uses{def:discrete_development}
  Let $n = 2m$ with $m \ge 1$, and let $\kappa, \ell : \ZMod n \to \mathbb{R}$
  satisfy the half-period symmetry $\kappa_{i+m} = \kappa_i$ and
  $\ell_{i+m} = \ell_i$ for all $i \in \ZMod n$, together with
  $\mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,\ell = 2\pi$. Then
  $\mathrm{closureGap}\,\kappa\,\ell = 0$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \uses{lem:turningangle_period, lem:half_block_sum, lem:heading_add_half}
  \leanok
  Recall $\mathrm{closureGap}\,\kappa\,\ell = \mathrm{vertexR2}\,\kappa\,\ell\,n
    = \sum_{j \in \mathrm{range}\,n} \ell_j\,e^{\,\mathrm{i}\,\psi_j}$ with
  $\psi_j = \mathrm{heading}\,\kappa\,\ell\,j$ (\cref{def:discrete_development}).
  Split $\mathrm{range}\,(2m) = \mathrm{range}\,m \sqcup \{m,\dots,2m-1\}$ by
  \texttt{Finset.sum\_range\_add}:
  \[
    \mathrm{closureGap}
      = \sum_{j \in \mathrm{range}\,m} \ell_j\,e^{\,\mathrm{i}\,\psi_j}
      + \sum_{j \in \mathrm{range}\,m} \ell_{j+m}\,e^{\,\mathrm{i}\,\psi_{j+m}} .
  \]
  For each $j$: the edge length is unchanged,
  $\ell_{(j+m : \mathbb{N})} = \ell_{(j : \mathbb{N})}$ by the half-period
  symmetry of $\ell$ (cast $(j+m : \mathbb{N}) = (j : \ZMod n) + (m : \ZMod n)$
  and apply $\ell_{i+m} = \ell_i$); and the heading advances by exactly $\pi$,
  \[
    \psi_{j+m} = \mathrm{heading}\,\kappa\,\ell\,(j+m)
      = \mathrm{heading}\,\kappa\,\ell\,j + \pi = \psi_j + \pi
  \]
  (\cref{lem:heading_add_half}). Hence
  $e^{\,\mathrm{i}\,\psi_{j+m}} = e^{\,\mathrm{i}\psi_j}\,e^{\,\mathrm{i}\pi}
    = -\,e^{\,\mathrm{i}\psi_j}$ (\texttt{Complex.exp\_pi\_mul\_I}), so the
  $(j+m)$-th edge vector is the negative of the $j$-th and the two sums cancel
  term by term, giving $\mathrm{closureGap} = 0$. (Verified numerically to
  $|\mathrm{gap}| \sim 10^{-16}$ by
  \texttt{references/verify\_symmetric\_bicircle.py}.)
\end{proof}

The proof rests on three atomic facts. Write $\theta_i = \mathrm{turningAngle}\,
0\,\kappa\,\ell\,i$.

\begin{lemma}\leanok
[turning angle is half-periodic]
  \label{lem:turningangle_period}
  \lean{Gluck.Discrete.turningAngle_add_half}
  \uses{def:discrete_development}
  Under the half-period symmetry $\kappa_{i+m} = \kappa_i$, $\ell_{i+m} = \ell_i$
  (all $i \in \ZMod n$, $n = 2m$), for every $i \in \ZMod n$,
  $\theta_{i+m} = \theta_i$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}

\leanok
  $\theta_i = \arcsin(\kappa_i\,\ell_{i-1}/2) + \arcsin(\kappa_i\,\ell_i/2)$
  (\cref{def:discrete_development}, $K=0$, $t_0(x)=x$). Substituting $i+m$ and
  using $\kappa_{i+m} = \kappa_i$, $\ell_{i+m} = \ell_i$, and
  $\ell_{(i+m)-1} = \ell_{(i-1)+m} = \ell_{i-1}$ gives $\theta_{i+m} = \theta_i$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}\leanok
[a half-period block of turning angles sums to $\pi$]
  \label{lem:half_block_sum}
  \lean{Gluck.Discrete.turningAngle_block_sum_half}
  \uses{lem:turningangle_period}
  Under the same hypotheses and $\mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa\,\ell = 2\pi$, for
  every $a \in \mathbb{N}$,
  $\sum_{i \in \mathrm{range}\,m} \theta_{(a+i : \ZMod n)} = \pi$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}

\leanok
  Let $B(a) = \sum_{i \in \mathrm{range}\,m}\theta_{(a+i:\ZMod n)}$. By
  \cref{lem:turningangle_period}, $\theta$ is invariant under the index shift by
  $m$, so $B(a+m) = B(a)$; hence the two consecutive $m$-blocks starting at $a$
  are equal. Their concatenation is a block of $n = 2m$ consecutive indices,
  whose sum equals $\sum_{i \in \ZMod n}\theta_i = \mathrm{turningSum} = 2\pi$
  by \texttt{sum\_range\_natCast\_add} (\cref{def:discrete_development}). Thus
  $2\,B(a) = 2\pi$, i.e.\ $B(a) = \pi$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}\leanok
[heading advances by $\pi$ over a half-period]
  \label{lem:heading_add_half}
  \lean{Gluck.Discrete.heading_add_half}
  \uses{lem:half_block_sum}
  Under the same hypotheses, for every $j \in \mathbb{N}$,
  $\mathrm{heading}\,\kappa\,\ell\,(j+m) = \mathrm{heading}\,\kappa\,\ell\,j + \pi$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}

\leanok
  $\mathrm{heading}\,\kappa\,\ell\,(j+m) = \sum_{i \in \mathrm{range}\,(j+m+1)}
    \theta_{(i:\ZMod n)}$. Rewrite $j+m+1 = (j+1)+m$ and split off the last $m$
  terms (\texttt{Finset.sum\_range\_add}):
  $\mathrm{heading}\,\kappa\,\ell\,(j+m) = \mathrm{heading}\,\kappa\,\ell\,j
    + \sum_{i \in \mathrm{range}\,m}\theta_{((j+1)+i : \ZMod n)}$. The trailing
  block is $\pi$ by \cref{lem:half_block_sum} with $a = j+1$.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}\leanok
[central-symmetrization and its homotopy]
  \label{def:central_symmetrization}
  \lean{Gluck.Discrete.centralSym}
  For a positive profile $\kappa$ ($n = 2m$) put
  $\kappa^0_i := \tfrac12(\kappa_i + \kappa_{i+m})$ (centrally symmetric,
  positive) and $\kappa_t := \kappa^0 + t(\kappa - \kappa^0)$, $t \in [0,1]$, so
  $\kappa_0 = \kappa^0$ and $\kappa_1 = \kappa$. All $\kappa_t$ are positive.
\end{definition}

\subsubsection*{Turning-angle coordinates and the antisymmetric $2$-cell}

The naive parametrization by edge lengths is the wrong chart: the constraint
$\mathrm{turningSum} = 2\pi$ is then a curved wall and the $t=0$ anchor is not a
disk center in the obvious way (iter-070 CAUTION --- the moderate slack collapses
toward the wall, so an edge-length margin is \textbf{false as stated}). The fix
(ChatGPT gpt-5.5 consult, 2026-07-14) is to change coordinates to the per-edge
turning contribution, which makes $\mathrm{turningSum} = 2\pi$ \emph{linear} and
gives an honest, $t$-independent $2$-disk.

\begin{definition}\leanok
[turning-angle chart]
  \label{def:turning_chart}
  \lean{Gluck.Discrete.chartMap}
  Fix $t \in [0,1]$. For each edge $j \in \ZMod n$ set
  \[
    \tau_{t,j}(x) := \arcsin(\kappa_{t,j}\,x/2) + \arcsin(\kappa_{t,j+1}\,x/2),
    \qquad x \in \bigl(0,\ 2/\max(\kappa_{t,j},\kappa_{t,j+1})\bigr),
  \]
  so that $\mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa_t\,\ell = \sum_j \tau_{t,j}(\ell_j)$ (each
  $\tau_{t,j}$ is the two-arcsin contribution of edge $j$ to the two turning
  angles at its endpoints). $\tau_{t,j}$ is a strictly increasing continuous
  bijection onto $(0,\beta_{t,j})$ with
  $\beta_{t,j} = \tfrac\pi2 + \arcsin\!\bigl(\min(\kappa_{t,j},\kappa_{t,j+1})/
  \max(\kappa_{t,j},\kappa_{t,j+1})\bigr) > \tfrac\pi2$; write $\lambda_{t,j} :=
  \tau_{t,j}^{-1}$ for its (continuous, strictly increasing) inverse. In the chart
  variables $s_j := \tau_{t,j}(\ell_j)$ the turning constraint is exactly the
  \emph{affine} condition $\sum_j s_j = 2\pi$, and $\ell_j = \lambda_{t,j}(s_j)$
  recovers the edge lengths inside the moderate domain $\Omega(\kappa_t)$.
\end{definition}

\emph{Lean encoding hint (mathlib-analogist @075, verdict PROCEED).} Package each
$\tau_{t,j}$ as a \texttt{Homeomorph} between the two open intervals via
\texttt{StrictMonoOn.orderIso} (with \texttt{StrictMonoOn.image\_Ioo\_eq} for the
range computation) then \texttt{OrderIso.toHomeomorph} (subtypes of $\mathbb{R}$
carry the order topology); $\lambda_{t,j} := (\cdot).\mathtt{invFun}$ is then
continuous strictly increasing with the round-trip identities for free. Keep
$\lambda$ \emph{explicit} --- it makes downstream continuity of $F$ a plain
composition. \textbf{Joint continuity} of $(t,z)\mapsto\Phi(t,z)$ is the one
nontrivial obligation: Mathlib has no one-shot ``inverse of a continuous
parametrized family is jointly continuous'' lemma. Since $\kappa_t = \kappa^0 +
t(\kappa-\kappa^0)$ is \emph{affine} (hence $C^1$) in $t$, the inverse-function
theorem route (\texttt{IsLocalHomeomorph.toHomeomorphOfBijective} on
$(t,x)\mapsto(t,\tau_t(x))$, Jacobian lower-triangular with positive diagonal
$\partial_x\tau_t>0$) gives joint continuity; alternatively a short bespoke
$\varepsilon$--$\delta$ lemma suffices. This is a dedicated \texttt{mathlib-build}
step preceding \cref{def:closing_2cell}.

\begin{definition}\leanok
[antisymmetric closing $2$-cell]
  \label{def:closing_2cell}
  \lean{Gluck.Discrete.closingCell}
  \uses{def:turning_chart, def:central_symmetrization, def:chart_inv,
        lem:chart_map_chart_inv, lem:chart_inv_mem, lem:chart_mem_image,
        lem:chart_wall, lem:continuous_inv_family, lem:curv_homotopy_pos,
        lem:continuous_curv_homotopy, def:chart_perturb, lem:sum_chart_perturb,
        lem:turning_sum_edge_chart}
  By compactness of $[0,1]$, $\underline\beta_j := \min_{t\in[0,1]}\beta_{t,j} >
  \tfrac\pi2$. Fix a half-period-symmetric chart base $s^0$ with $s^0_{j+m} =
  s^0_j$, $0 < s^0_j < \underline\beta_j$ and $\sum_j s^0_j = 2\pi$ (possible since
  each $\underline\beta_j > \tfrac\pi2$; e.g.\ $s^0_j = 2\pi/n$ when all
  $\underline\beta_j > 2\pi/n$). Choose two half-pair indices $a,b \in
  \{0,\dots,m-1\}$ and a radius
  $\rho < \min_{k\in\{a,a+m,b,b+m\}}\{s^0_k,\ \underline\beta_k - s^0_k\}$. For
  $z = (u,v) \in \overline{D}_\rho \subset \mathbb{R}^2$ define the chart point
  \[
    s(z)_a = s^0_a + u,\quad s(z)_{a+m} = s^0_a - u,\quad
    s(z)_b = s^0_b + v,\quad s(z)_{b+m} = s^0_b - v,
  \]
  all other $s(z)_j = s^0_j$. Then $\sum_j s(z)_j = 2\pi$ \emph{identically} in
  $z$, every $s(z)_k \in (0,\underline\beta_k)$, and
  \[
    \Phi(t,z)_j := \lambda_{t,j}\bigl(s(z)_j\bigr)
  \]
  is a continuous map $[0,1]\times\overline{D}_\rho \to \Omega(\kappa_t)$ with
  $\mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa_t\,\Phi(t,z) = 2\pi$ throughout. The center
  $\Phi(t,0)$ has chart coordinates $s^0$ (half-period symmetric), so at $t=0$,
  $\Phi(0,0)$ is a \emph{half-period-symmetric} edge-length vector (because
  $\lambda_{0,j} = \lambda_{0,j+m}$ under $\kappa_0 = \kappa^0$ symmetric), hence
  closes by \cref{lem:central_symmetry_closes}. Set
  $F(t,z) := \mathrm{closureGap}\,\kappa_t\,\Phi(t,z) \in \mathbb{C}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[why edge-length coordinates and the single-edge tune are retired here]
  \label{rem:chart_supersedes_edge_tune}
  The chart of \cref{def:turning_chart} supersedes the raw edge-length packaging:
  the affine constraint $\sum_j s_j = 2\pi$ makes the dependent-coordinate solve
  (the \cref{sec:edge_tune} single-edge tune \cref{lem:exists_edge_turning_scale})
  unnecessary for the closing family --- the antisymmetric variation keeps
  $\sum s_j = 2\pi$ for free. \Cref{sec:edge_tune} remains a correct, axiom-clean
  monotone-tune API but is off this critical path.
\end{remark}

\subsubsection*{The crux, decomposed into one provable and one open leaf}

The winding-continuation argument needs exactly three facts about $F$:
(i) continuity, (ii) $F(0,\cdot)$ winds $\pm1$ on $\partial D_\rho$, (iii) $F \ne 0$
on $[0,1]\times\partial D_\rho$. The ChatGPT consult (2026-07-14) reduced (ii) to a
\emph{rigidity} lemma (below, \textbf{provable} --- the discrete Prop~9.1 ``only
if'' direction) and isolated (iii) as the single genuinely open analytic leaf.

\begin{lemma}[$t=0$ closing rigidity on the $2$-cell --- discrete Prop~9.1 ``only if'']

  \cref{def:closing_2cell}, and the two perturbed half-pairs \emph{distinct}
  ($a \ne b$ and $a \ne b + m$, so $\{a, a{+}m\} \cap \{b, b{+}m\} =

  as its \emph{unique} zero on $\overline{D}_\rho$. (Lean realization: the

\begin{proof}

\begin{lemma}[winding $\pm1$ at $t=0$]
  \label{lem:closure_winding_t0}
  \uses{lem:closure_boundary_rigidity, def:closing_2cell}
  $F(0,\cdot)|_{\partial D_\rho}$ has winding number $\pm1$ about $0$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \uses{lem:closure_boundary_rigidity}
  By \cref{lem:closure_boundary_rigidity} the only zero of the continuous map
  $F(0,\cdot)$ on $\overline{D}_\rho$ is the interior point $z=0$, and its closure
  Jacobian $DF(0,\cdot)|_{z=0}$ is nonsingular. Hence $z=0$ is an isolated
  nondegenerate zero with local degree $\mathrm{sgn}\det DF = \pm1$, and (no other
  zero in $\overline{D}_\rho$) excision identifies this with the winding number of
  the outer boundary loop $F(0,\cdot)|_{\partial D_\rho}$, which is therefore
  $\pm1$. \emph{(In Lean this is the outer-boundary specialization of the in-tree
  \texttt{exists\_zero\_of\_boundary\_winding} degree engine,
  \cref{chap:winding}.)}
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[boundary exclusion along the homotopy --- OPEN analytic leaf]
  % NOTE (review @079): the constant-κ⁰ degeneracy refuting

  \label{lem:closure_boundary_exclusion}
  \uses{def:closing_2cell}
  For $\rho$ as in \cref{def:closing_2cell} and every $t \in [0,1]$,
  $z \in \partial D_\rho$: $F(t,z) \ne 0$.
\end{lemma}

\emph{Status of \cref{lem:closure_boundary_exclusion} --- the one genuinely open
leaf (updated @075).} At $t=0$ this is \cref{lem:closure_boundary_rigidity}
($z \ne 0 \Rightarrow F(0,z) \ne 0$); by compactness $\min_{z\in\partial D_\rho}
|F(0,z)| =: \delta_\rho > 0$. For small $t$ it follows by uniform continuity
($|F(t,z) - F(0,z)| < \delta_\rho$). \textbf{The open content is boundary
exclusion for the full $t \in [0,1]$}, where the margin $\delta_\rho$ need not
beat the total variation of $F$ in $t$. Numerically this holds: the
boundary-exclusion probe (\texttt{references/verify\_boundary\_exclusion.py}, @072)
found, across $n \in \{4,6,8,10,12\}$, $10$ DFV profiles each and a $6$-point
$t$-grid, worst-case $\min|F|$ on $\partial\Omega\cap\{\mathrm{turningSum}=2\pi\}$
equal to $0.0090$ ($\sim6$ orders above numerical zero). \textbf{Dead end recorded
(do not retry):} the support-function / ``all headings in an arc of length
$<2\pi$'' argument \emph{cannot} prove this --- positivity
$\mathrm{Re}(e^{-\mathrm{i}\alpha}F) = \sum_j \ell_j\cos(\psi_j-\alpha) > 0$ needs
all edge directions in an arc of length $<\pi$, but the shortest arc containing all
$n$ directions has length $2\pi - \max_i\theta_i > \pi$ (the cyclic gaps are the
$\theta_i \in (0,\pi)$ and $\sum\theta_i = 2\pi$), which is incompatible with any
$2\pi$-turning closed development (ChatGPT gpt-5.5, 2026-07-14). A rigorous proof
must instead be a boundary-tolerant degree/interval-certification argument;
\textbf{no prover is dispatched on \cref{lem:closure_boundary_exclusion} until such
an argument is authored to atomic detail and a HARD GATE clears it}.

\begin{lemma}[closure persists along the homotopy --- CRUX, modulo the open leaf]
  \label{lem:closure_persists}
  \uses{lem:closure_winding_t0, lem:closure_boundary_exclusion}
  For $n \ge 4$ even and $\kappa$ with $\mathrm{DiscreteFourVertexPos}$, the
  closing solution at $t = 0$ (\cref{lem:central_symmetry_closes}) continues to a
  closing solution at $t = 1$: for every $t \in [0,1]$ there is $z_t \in D_\rho$
  with $F(t,z_t) = 0$, so $\ell_t := \Phi(t,z_t)$ is moderate-arc with
  $\mathrm{turningSum}\,\kappa_t\,\ell_t = 2\pi$ and
  $\mathrm{closureGap}\,\kappa_t\,\ell_t = 0$; at $t=1$, $\ell_1$ realizes $\kappa$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  $F$ is continuous on $[0,1]\times\overline{D}_\rho$ (\cref{def:closing_2cell}).
  By \cref{lem:closure_boundary_exclusion}, $F(t,\cdot)$ is zero-free on
  $\partial D_\rho$ for all $t$, so $t \mapsto F(t,\cdot)|_{\partial D_\rho}$ is a
  homotopy of zero-avoiding loops; by homotopy-invariance of the winding number
  (in-tree \texttt{windingNumber\_eq\_of\_homotopy}, needs a public wrapper) the
  boundary winding is constant in $t$, hence equals its $t=0$ value $\pm1$
  (\cref{lem:closure_winding_t0}) for every $t$. By the in-tree engine
  \texttt{exists\_zero\_of\_boundary\_winding} (\cref{chap:winding}: nonzero
  boundary winding $\Rightarrow$ interior zero) each $F(t,\cdot)$ has a zero
  $z_t \in D_\rho$. Then $\ell_t = \Phi(t,z_t)$ is moderate with
  $\mathrm{turningSum} = 2\pi$ (\cref{def:closing_2cell}) and $\mathrm{closureGap}
  = F(t,z_t) = 0$; at $t=1$, $\kappa_1 = \kappa$, so $\ell_1$ realizes $\kappa$.
\end{proof}

\subsection*{Route B (alternative): bicircle configuration winding}

Source-faithful to Gluck: reduce a general $\mathrm{DFV}$ profile to a discrete
\emph{bicircle} (four arcs / two curvature levels $a > c > b$ at the window level
$c$), for which the error vector has an explicit closed form and the
configuration loop winds $\pm 1$.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Bicircle core + explicit error.} Build the discrete $4$-arc
    bicircle and its error vector; the smooth model gives
    $E = (1/ib - 1/ia)(1 - q_2 + q_3 - q_4)$ (deturck-gluck Prop~15.2), vanishing
    exactly on the symmetric ``core'' $q_1 - q_2 + q_3 - q_4 = 0$ (Prop~9.1).
    This mirrors the in-tree smooth \texttt{Bicircle.lean} / \texttt{Winding.lean}
    engine (\cref{chap:winding}: \texttt{errorMap\_winding\_eq\_one},
    \texttt{exists\_zero\_of\_boundary\_winding}).
  \item \textbf{Transversality $\Rightarrow$ winding $\pm 1$.} The differential of
    the error map is nonsingular on the core (Prop~15.2), forcing winding $\pm 1$
    of the configuration loop (Prop~15.1). This step is a direct discrete analogue
    and appears tractable (strategy-auditor).
  \item \textbf{General $\to$ bicircle transfer (NOVEL).} Deform a general
    $\mathrm{DFV}$ profile to the $4$-arc bicircle model (e.g.\ merging
    adjacent same-sign turning). This step is \emph{not} in Gluck (no direct
    analogue of his C$^0$-density / Prop~17.1 evaluation map, since the discrete
    $\kappa$ is an exact target, not an approximand) --- it is the open research
    step, the same difficulty as \cref{lem:closure_persists}.
\end{itemize}
